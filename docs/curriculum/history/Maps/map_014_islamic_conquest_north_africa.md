# Map 14: Islamic Conquest of North Africa
## Arab Expansion, Byzantine Collapse, and Berber Resistance (640-710 AD)

---

## ERA & CHRONOLOGY

**Young Earth Dating:** c. 640–710 AD (Early Islamic Period)  
**Biblical Framework:**
-   **Post-Apostolic Era:** The decline of the North African church (once the home of Augustine, Cyprian, Tertullian).
-   **Islamic Expansion:** The "Locusts" of Revelation 9 (historicist interpretation often links this to Arab conquests, though YEC focus is on historical reality).
-   **End of Antiquity:** Transition from Roman/Byzantine rule to Islamic Caliphate.

**Conventional Equivalent:** c. 640–710 AD (Rashidun and Umayyad Caliphates)

**Key Dates:**
-   **642 AD** — Conquest of Egypt complete (Amr ibn al-As).
-   **647 AD** — Battle of Sufetula (Sbeitla); Defeat of Byzantine Exarch Gregory.
-   **670 AD** — Founding of Kairouan by Uqba ibn Nafi (first permanent Arab base).
-   **681-683 AD** — Uqba ibn Nafi's "Grand Raid" to the Atlantic; Death at Vescera.
-   **698 AD** — Battle of Carthage; Final Byzantine expulsion.
-   **695-703 AD** — Berber resistance under Queen Kahina (Dihya).
-   **711 AD** — Launch of invasion into Spain (Tariq ibn Ziyad).

**Chronology Note:**
YEC and conventional timelines align for this period. The focus is on the *interpretation* of the speed of religious change (see Conflicts & Resolutions).

## BIBLICAL & CHURCH HISTORY FRAMEWORK

### The "Removed Lampstand" (Theological Reflection)
**Revelation 2:5** — "Repent and do the works you did at first. If not, I will come to you and remove your lampstand from its place."
-   **Context:** North Africa was a powerhouse of theology (Augustine, Cyprian).
-   **Internal Weakness:** The church was fractured by schisms (Donatist vs. Catholic), heresies (Arian Vandals), and fatigue from Byzantine persecution of Monophysites.
-   **Historical Result:** Unlike the Coptic Church in Egypt (which survived as a strong minority), the North African church largely disappeared over the next few centuries.

### The Flight of Christians
-   **Refugees:** Many Greek-speaking elites and clergy fled to Sicily, Italy, and Spain.
-   **Remnant:** Archaeological evidence shows small Christian communities persisted (e.g., Kairouan inscriptions, local bishops) but gradually dwindled under *dhimmi* status (taxation).

### Biblical Geography Connection
-   **Libya/Cyrene:** Once the home of Simon of Cyrene (Mark 15:21) and Pentecost pilgrims (Acts 2:10), now absorbed into the *Dar al-Islam*.

-   **North:** Mediterranean Sea (38°N, including Sicily/Sardinia for context)
-   **South:** Sahara Desert Edge (28°N, Fezzan/Ghadames)

**1. Ifriqiya (Africa Proconsularis):**
-   **Heartland:** Modern Tunisia.
-   **Capital Shift:** From Carthage (coastal/Byzantine) to Kairouan (inland/Arab).
-   **Terrain:** Coastal plains (Tell) vs. Steppe (Sahel).

**2. Maghreb al-Awsat (Central Maghreb):**
-   **Region:** Modern Algeria.
-   **Key Sites:** Tahert, Tlemcen, Aurès Mountains (Berber strongholds).

**3. Maghreb al-Aqsa (Far West):**
-   **Region:** Modern Morocco.
-   **Key Sites:** Tangier, Volubilis (Walila), Sus region.

**4. The Desert Frontier:**
-   **Fezzan:** Conquered early (666 AD) by Uqba ibn Nafi to secure the southern flank.

## MAJOR CITIES & SITES

### KAIROUAN (Tunisia)
**Coordinates:** 35.6793°N, 10.0963°E  
**Significance:**
-   **Founded:** c. 670 AD by Uqba ibn Nafi.
-   **Role:** First Islamic capital in Maghreb; military base (*misr*) for westward expansion.
-   **Site:** Chosen to be inland (safe from Byzantine navy) and near pasture for cavalry.

### CARTHAGE (Tunisia)
**Coordinates:** 36.8529°N, 10.3225°E  
**Significance:**
-   **Byzantine Capital:** Stronghold of Exarchate of Africa.
-   **Fall:** Destroyed/captured in 698 AD by Hassan ibn al-Nu'man.
-   **Fate:** Stones reused to build Tunis; eclipsed by Kairouan.

### SUFETULA (Sbeitla, Tunisia)
**Coordinates:** 35.2297°N, 9.1294°E  
**Significance:**
-   **Battle of Sufetula (647 AD):** First major Arab victory.
-   **Gregory the Patrician:** Byzantine governor who declared independence, killed in battle.

### VESCERA (Biskra/Sidi Okba, Algeria)
**Coordinates:** 34.8500°N, 5.7333°E  
**Significance:**
-   **Battle of Vescera (682 AD):** Ambush by Berber King Kusaila.
-   **Death of Uqba:** Uqba ibn Nafi killed here; halted Arab expansion for a decade.

### TANGIER (Morocco)
**Coordinates:** 35.7595°N, 5.8330°W  
**Significance:**
-   **Atlantic Terminus:** Reached by Uqba (c. 682 AD).
-   **Staging Ground:** Later base for invasion of Spain (711 AD).

## PEOPLES & POPULATIONS

### The Conquerors (Arabs)
-   **Umayyad Caliphate:** Based in Damascus.
-   **Generals:** Amr ibn al-As, Uqba ibn Nafi, Hassan ibn al-Nu'man, Musa ibn Nusayr.
-   **Motivation:** Expansion of *Dar al-Islam*, booty, tribute (*jizya*).

### The Defenders (Byzantines/Rum)
-   **Exarchate of Africa:** Weakened by internal revolts and religious disputes.
-   **Navy:** Controlled the sea (reason for inland Arab bases like Kairouan).

### The Resistors (Berbers/Amazigh)
-   **Awraba Tribe:** Led by **Kusaila** (Christian Berber king). Defeated Uqba.
-   **Jarawa Tribe:** Led by **Queen Kahina (Dihya)** (Jewish or Christian Berber queen). Led fierce resistance in Aurès Mts.
-   **Shift:** Initially resisted, later converted and became the bulk of the army invading Spain.

## HISTORICAL SOURCES & EVIDENCE

### Islamic Chronicles (Futuh)
-   **Ibn Abd al-Hakam (9th c.):** *Conquest of Egypt and North Africa*. Primary source for Uqba's raid.
-   **Al-Baladhuri:** *Book of the Conquests of Lands*.

### Christian/Byzantine Sources
-   **Chronicle of John of Nikiu:** Coptic perspective on the conquest of Egypt.
-   **Archaeology:**
    -   **Sbeitla:** Destruction layers dated to mid-7th century.
    -   **Kairouan Great Mosque:** Oldest minaret in the West (lower levels date to 8th c.).
    -   **Coins:** Transition from Byzantine solidus to Islamic dinar (Arab-Byzantine transitional coinage).

## CONFLICTS & RESOLUTIONS

### Conflict 1: Rapid Conquest vs. Gradual Conversion
**Conventional/Popular View:** "Sword of Islam" forced immediate conversion.
**Historical Reality:**
-   **Conquest (Military):** Rapid (640-710 AD).
-   **Conversion (Religious):** Very gradual. Berbers resisted fiercely.
-   **YEC/Biblical Nuance:** The "disappearance" of the church was not instantaneous. It was a slow strangulation due to:
    1.  **Taxation:** *Jizya* tax on non-Muslims.
    2.  **Emigration:** Elites fled to Europe.
    3.  **Isolation:** Cut off from Rome and Constantinople.
**Resolution:** Map should show *military* control, not total religious homogeneity.

### Conflict 2: The "Grand Raid" of Uqba — Fact or Legend?
**Critique:** Some modern historians doubt Uqba reached the Atlantic or rode his horse into the waves.
**YEC/Traditional Approach:**
-   We accept the core historical narrative of the chronicles unless contradicted by hard evidence.
-   **Resolution:** Map the route to Tangier and the Atlantic as the "Traditional Route of Uqba," acknowledging it as the high-water mark of the first wave.

### Conflict 3: Kahina's Religion — Jewish or Christian?
**Debate:** Arabic sources call her Jewish; some scholars suggest Christian or Pagan.
**Resolution:** Label her as leader of the "Jarawa Berbers" (Zenata). Note in text: "Likely a mix of indigenous Berber religion and Judaism/Christianity."

## PEDAGOGICAL NOTES

### Discussion Questions
1.  **The Disappearing Church:** Why did Christianity survive in Egypt (Copts) under Islamic rule but largely disappear in North Africa (Maghreb)? Discuss the role of indigenous language (Coptic vs. Latin) and church unity.
2.  **Resistance & Conversion:** How did the Berbers go from being the fiercest enemies of the Arabs (under Kahina) to being the primary soldiers in the invasion of Spain (under Tariq ibn Ziyad) in just a few decades?
3.  **Biblical Judgment:** Read Revelation 2:5. Can we view the collapse of the North African church as a removal of its "lampstand" due to centuries of infighting (Donatists, Vandals)? How does this warn the modern church?
4.  **Geography of Conquest:** Why did Uqba ibn Nafi build Kairouan inland, away from the coast? (Answer: To avoid the Byzantine navy). How does geography dictate military strategy?

### Classroom Activities
1.  **"The Grand Raid" Tracing:** Have students trace Uqba ibn Nafi's 2,000-mile journey on a blank map. Calculate the distance and discuss the logistics of moving an army that far across desert and mountain terrain.
2.  **Primary Source Comparison:** Read an account of the conquest from an Arab chronicler (Ibn Abd al-Hakam) and a Christian one (John of Nikiu). Compare their descriptions of the conquerors.
