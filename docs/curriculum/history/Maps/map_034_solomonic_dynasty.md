# Map 34: Solomonic Dynasty Ethiopia (1270-1500 AD)
## The Lion of Judah: Restoration, Expansion & The Kebra Nagast

---

## ERA & CHRONOLOGY

**Young Earth Dating:** 1270 AD – 1500 AD (Late Medieval)  
**Biblical Framework:**
- **The Covenant Line:** The Solomonic Dynasty claimed direct descent from King Solomon and the Queen of Sheba (1 Kings 10), viewing themselves as "Israel in Africa."
- **Prophetic Fulfillment:** Psalm 87:4 ("I will make mention of... Ethiopia... this one was born there"). Interpreted as a spiritual birthright.

**Conventional Equivalent:** 1270 AD – 1500 AD (Solomonic Period)  
**Key Chronological Markers:**
- **1270 AD:** **Yekuno Amlak** overthrows the last Zagwe king, "restoring" the Solomonic line.
- **1314-1344 AD:** Reign of **Amda Seyon I** (The Warrior King). Massive expansion, conquest of Islamic sultanates.
- **1434-1468 AD:** Reign of **Zara Yaqob** (The Theologian King). Church reform, literary renaissance.
- **1490s AD:** Arrival of Portuguese envoys (Pero da Covilhã). Search for "Prester John."
- **1529 AD:** (Post-map context) Invasion of Ahmed Gragn (Adal Sultanate).

## BIBLICAL & CHURCH HISTORY FRAMEWORK

### Scriptural Foundation
**1 Kings 10:1-13 (Solomon & Sheba):**
- **The Foundation:** The biblical account of the Queen's visit is the cornerstone of Ethiopian national identity.
- **Ethiopian Addition:** The *Kebra Nagast* ("Glory of Kings") claims she bore Solomon a son, **Menelik I**, who brought the Ark of the Covenant to Ethiopia.

**Revelation 5:5:** "Behold, the Lion of the tribe of Judah, the Root of David, has prevailed."
- **Royal Title:** The Ethiopian Emperors used the title "Conquering Lion of the Tribe of Judah," linking their throne to Christ's messianic lineage.

**Psalm 72:10:** "The kings of Sheba and Seba shall offer gifts."
- **Application:** Viewed as a prophecy of Ethiopia's Christian destiny.

### Theological Theme: "Chosen People"
- **Replacement Theology?** Not exactly. Ethiopia viewed itself as the *successor* to Israel (possessing the Ark) and the *defender* of Christianity in a sea of Islam.
- **Judaic Christianity:** They maintained Sabbath observance (Saturday), circumcision, dietary laws, and Temple-style worship (Tabots), believing they preserved the "original" faith of the Apostles which was Jewish in nature.

## TECHNICAL SECTION: ETHIOPIAN ORTHODOX DISTINCTIVES

### 1. The Kebra Nagast ("The Glory of Kings")
- **Nature:** The national epic/constitution, compiled c. 1320 AD.
- **Claim:** The Ark of the Covenant was moved from Jerusalem to Aksum because Israel lost God's favor due to idolatry. Ethiopia is the new Zion.
- **Impact:** Legitimized the Solomonic Dynasty for 700 years (until 1974).

### 2. The Tabot (Ark Replica)
- **Concept:** Every Ethiopian church must have a *Tabot* (a replica of the Tablets of Law) on the altar to be consecrated.
- **Significance:** The church building is a Temple; the Holy of Holies contains the Tabot.
- **Uniqueness:** No other Christian tradition centers its liturgy on the Ark of the Covenant.

### 3. Sabbath Observance
- **The Controversy:** The "Ewostathians" (followers of St. Ewostatewos) insisted on keeping *both* Saturday (Sabbath) and Sunday (Lord's Day).
- **Resolution:** Council of Debre Mitmaq (1450 AD, under Zara Yaqob) affirmed the two-Sabbath rule.

## GEOGRAPHIC COVERAGE

### Map Boundaries
- **North:** Massawa / Red Sea [~16°N]
- **South:** Lake Abaya / Bale Mountains [~6°N]
- **East:** Harar / Zeila (Adal Sultanate) [~44°E]
- **West:** Blue Nile / Sudan Border [~34°E]

### Geographic Zones
1.  **The Christian Highlands (Abyssinia):** Amhara, Tigray, Shewa, Gojjam. The core empire.
2.  **The Islamic Lowlands (The Ring of Fire):** Ifat, Adal, Dawaro, Hadiya. The "hostile" sultanates surrounding the plateau.
3.  **The Mobile Capital:** The Solomonic kings had no fixed capital (like Gondar later). They lived in massive **Royal Camps** (*Ketema*) that moved around to consume resources and project power.

## MAJOR CITIES & SITES

### 1. Tegulet (Shewa)
- **Coords:** 9.80°N, 39.40°E
- **Role:** Early capital of the Solomonic dynasty (Yekuno Amlak).

### 2. Debre Libanos
- **Coords:** 9.71°N, 38.85°E
- **Role:** Major monastery founded by St. Tekle Haymanot. Center of the "Ethiopian" monastic movement.

### 3. Gishen Mariam (Amba Gishen)
- **Coords:** 11.67°N, 39.50°E
- **Role:** The "Royal Prison" (Amba). Male heirs to the throne were locked on this flat-topped mountain to prevent civil war.
- **Relic:** Claimed to house a piece of the True Cross (Gishen Mariam).

### 4. Lake Tana Monasteries
- **Coords:** 12.00°N, 37.30°E
- **Role:** Island monasteries (Daga Estifanos, Tana Qirqos). Burial place of kings.

### 5. Harar (Islamic Center)
- **Coords:** 9.31°N, 42.12°E
- **Role:** Holy city of Islam in the Horn. Base for jihad against Ethiopia.

## PEOPLES & POPULATIONS

### 1. The Amhara
- **Identity:** Semitic-speaking highlanders. The ruling class of the Solomonic dynasty.
- **Culture:** Warrior-farmers, deeply Orthodox.

### 2. The Tigrayans
- **Identity:** Northern Semitic speakers (heirs of Aksum).

### 3. The Sidama & Oromo (South)
- **Identity:** Cushitic peoples. At this stage, mostly pagan/traditional religion, raided for slaves/tribute by the Empire.

### 4. The Walashma Dynasty (Ifat/Adal)
- **Identity:** Muslim rulers of the eastern trade routes. Perennial enemies of the Solomonic kings.

## HISTORICAL SOURCES & EVIDENCE

### Royal Chronicles
- **The Glorious Victories of Amda Seyon:** Detailed account of his campaigns in 1329 AD. Describes the geography, enemy troop numbers, and the King's personal bravery.
- **The Chronicle of Zara Yaqob:** Documents his theological books (*Motsahafe Milad*) and church reforms.

### European Accounts
- **Francisco Alvares:** *The Prester John of the Indies* (1520s). Detailed eyewitness account of the court, the moving capital, and the liturgy.

## CONFLICTS & RESOLUTIONS

### Conflict 1: The Kebra Nagast (History or Myth?)
**The Conflict:**
- **Skeptic:** Menelik I is a myth. No evidence Solomon had a son with Sheba.
- **Believer:** It is absolute history. The Ark is in Aksum.

**YEC/Biblical Resolution:**
- **Legendary Accretion:** The *Kebra Nagast* (14th c.) likely mythologized earlier traditions to legitimize the new dynasty (anti-Zagwe propaganda).
- **Core Truth:** While the biological link to Solomon may be legendary, the *spiritual* link is real. Ethiopia *did* adopt the God of Israel.
- **The Ark:** Whether the physical box is there or not, the *concept* of the Ark (God's presence) is central to their faith. We treat it as a "National Tradition" (like King Arthur) that shaped history, without endorsing its literal accuracy regarding Solomon.

### Conflict 2: Just War (Amda Seyon's Crusades)
**The Conflict:**
- Amda Seyon waged brutal wars against Muslim sultanates.
- Is this consistent with Christianity?

**Resolution:**
- **Defensive Imperialism:** Ethiopia was surrounded. Ifat and Adal constantly raided for slaves. Amda Seyon viewed his wars as defensive protection of the Church.
- **Old Testament Model:** They viewed themselves as Israel fighting the Philistines. They followed the OT "Holy War" model rather than the NT "Turn the Cheek" model.

### Conflict 3: Sabbath Observance (Judaizing?)
**The Conflict:**
- Why do they keep the Jewish Sabbath?
- **Catholic/Protestant View:** This is "Judaizing" (Galatians error).

**Resolution:**
- **Early Origins:** Christianity arrived in Ethiopia *early* (Acts 8), possibly before the Gentile/Roman church abandoned Jewish customs. They preserve a "Pre-Constantinian" or "Jewish-Christian" form of the faith.
- **Romans 14:** "One person esteems one day as better than another... let each be fully convinced in his own mind."

### Conflict 4: The "Prester John" Myth
**The Conflict:**
- Europe believed a mighty Christian King ("Prester John") lived in the East and would help them fight Islam.
- **Reality:** When Portuguese arrived, they found Ethiopia was Christian but weak and needing *help*, not able to *give* it.

**Resolution:**
- **Mutual Disappointment:** Europeans considered Ethiopians "heretics" (Monophysite). Ethiopians considered Europeans "idolaters" (images/statues) and "Sabbath-breakers."
- **Missed Opportunity:** Pride prevented a true alliance.

## PEDAGOGICAL NOTES

### Discussion Questions
1.  **National Myth:** How did the *Kebra Nagast* story help unify Ethiopia? Do nations need "origin stories"? (Compare to the Aeneid for Rome or the Mayflower for the USA).
2.  **Church & State:** The Emperor was the "Head of the Church." How does this compare to the Byzantine model? What are the dangers of mixing politics and faith?
3.  **Defensive War:** Was Amda Seyon right to wage war to protect his people? Read Romans 13:4 ("He does not bear the sword in vain").

### Activity: "The Royal Prison" (Debate)
-   **Topic:** The custom of locking all royal princes on a flat-topped mountain (Amba Gishen) to prevent civil war.
-   **Debate:**
    -   *Pro:* It stopped wars of succession and saved thousands of lives.
    -   *Con:* It is cruel to imprison innocent men for life.
-   **Context:** This inspired Samuel Johnson's *Rasselas*.

### Activity: "Tabot Procession"
-   **Video:** Watch a video of the *Timkat* (Epiphany) festival in Ethiopia today.
-   **Observation:** Note the dancing, the drums, the umbrellas, and the reverence for the Tabot. This is a living window into the Solomonic era.
