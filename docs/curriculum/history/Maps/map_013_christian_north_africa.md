# Map 13: Christian North Africa (Cities Focus) (c. 100-700 AD)
## The Era of Augustine, Donatist Schism, and Vandal Persecution

---

## 1. ERA & CHRONOLOGY

**Young Earth Dating:** c. 100 AD – 700 AD (Apostolic Era to Islamic Conquest)
**Biblical Framework:**
- **Apostolic Foundation:** c. 33-100 AD (Acts 2, Simon of Cyrene)
- **Age of Martyrs:** c. 180-313 AD (Persecution under Severus, Decius, Diocletian)
- **Golden Age:** c. 313-430 AD (Constantine to Death of Augustine)
- **Vandal/Arian Crisis:** c. 429-533 AD (Persecution of Nicene Church)
- **Byzantine Restoration:** c. 533-647 AD (Justinian's Reconquest)
- **Islamic Conquest:** c. 647-698 AD (Fall of Carthage)

**Conventional Equivalent:** Same dates (Church history is well-documented)

**Key Dates:**
- **180 AD** — **Scillitan Martyrs:** First record of Latin Christianity in the world.
- **203 AD** — **Martyrdom of Perpetua and Felicity:** A noblewoman and her slave die together in the Carthage arena.
- **258 AD** — **Martyrdom of Cyprian:** Bishop of Carthage beheaded.
- **311 AD** — **Beginning of Donatist Schism:** Dispute over the election of Caecilian as bishop.
- **354 AD** — Birth of Augustine in Thagaste.
- **395-430 AD** — Augustine as Bishop of Hippo. Writes *Confessions* and *City of God*.
- **411 AD** — **Council of Carthage:** The Great Conference (286 Catholic vs 279 Donatist bishops).
- **429 AD** — **Vandal Invasion:** Genseric leads 80,000 Vandals from Spain into Africa.
- **430 AD** — Death of Augustine during the Siege of Hippo.
- **533 AD** — Belisarius defeats Vandals; Africa returns to Roman (Byzantine) rule.
- **698 AD** — Carthage falls to Hassan ibn al-Nu'man; end of organized Latin Christianity.

## 2. BIBLICAL & CHURCH HISTORY FRAMEWORK

### New Testament Roots

**Acts 2:10 — The Pentecost Witness:**
> "...Phrygia and Pamphylia, Egypt and the parts of Libya adjoining Cyrene..."
- **Significance:** The Gospel reached North Africa on Day 1. The "Libyans" present likely returned to Cyrene and Carthage as the first evangelists.

**Mark 15:21 — Simon of Cyrene:**
> "Then they compelled a certain man, Simon a Cyrenian, the father of Alexander and Rufus..."
- **Legacy:** The mention of his sons suggests they were well-known in the early church (possibly the Rufus of Romans 16:13).

**Acts 13:1 — Lucius of Cyrene:**
> "Now in the church that was at Antioch there were certain prophets and teachers: Barnabas, Simeon who was called Niger, Lucius of Cyrene..."
- **Leadership:** North Africans were among the leaders of the Antioch church that sent out Paul.

### Theological Significance

**The Cradle of Latin Theology:**
- While the East debated philosophy (Greek), North Africa focused on **Law, Sin, and Grace** (Latin).
- **Tertullian:** The "Father of Latin Theology." Coined "Trinity" (*Trinitas*). Famous for: "The blood of the martyrs is the seed of the church."
- **Cyprian:** Defined the nature of the Church ("You cannot have God for your Father unless you have the Church for your mother").
- **Augustine:** The giant of Western theology. His battles against Manichaeism, Donatism, and Pelagianism defined the doctrines of Original Sin, Predestination, and the Sacraments.

## 3. GEOGRAPHIC COVERAGE

### Map Boundaries
- **North:** 38.0° N (Mediterranean)
- **South:** 32.0° N (Atlas foothills/Limes)
- **West:** 4.0° E (Numidia/Algeria)
- **East:** 12.0° E (Cap Bon/Tunisia)

### Geographic Regions

**1. Africa Proconsularis (The Catholic Core):**
- **Region:** Northern Tunisia (Medjerda Valley).
- **Character:** Urban, Romanized, Latin-speaking. Strongly Catholic (Nicene).
- **Key Cities:** Carthage, Utica, Thugga.

**2. Numidia (The Donatist Heartland):**
- **Region:** Eastern Algeria (High Plateaus).
- **Character:** Rural, Berber-speaking, fiercely independent. Strongly Donatist.
- **Key Cities:** Cirta, Timgad, Bagai, Thagaste.

**3. Byzacena (The Olive Belt):**
- **Region:** Central Tunisia (Sahel).
- **Character:** Mixed loyalties. Wealthy olive estates.
- **Key Cities:** Sufetula, Thysdrus (El Djem).

**4. Mauretania Sitifensis:**
- **Region:** Western frontier.
- **Character:** Heavily militarized.
- **Key City:** Sitifis.

## 4. MAJOR CITIES & SITES

### CARTHAGE (Tunis)
**Coordinates:** 36.85°N, 10.32°E
**Significance:**
- **Primate See:** The "Rome of Africa." The Bishop of Carthage was the de facto Patriarch of Africa.
- **Sites:** *Basilica Maiorum* (burial of Perpetua), *Damous el Karita* (massive pilgrimage basilica).
- **Councils:** Site of over 20 major church councils (251-419 AD), deciding the canon of Scripture (397 AD).

### HIPPO REGIUS (Annaba)
**Coordinates:** 36.90°N, 7.76°E
**Significance:**
- **Augustine's See:** Where he preached for 35 years.
- **Basilica of Peace:** Augustine's cathedral.
- **Library:** Where he wrote *City of God* while Vandals besieged the walls.
- **Council of Hippo (393 AD):** First council to list the 27 books of the NT.

### THAGASTE (Souk Ahras)
**Coordinates:** 36.28°N, 7.95°E
**Significance:**
- **Augustine's Birthplace:** A small backwater town.
- **Monasticism:** Where Augustine founded his first monastery after his conversion.
- **Alypius:** Augustine's lifelong friend and bishop of Thagaste.

### CIRTA (Constantine)
**Coordinates:** 36.36°N, 6.61°E
**Significance:**
- **Donatist Capital:** The origin of the schism (election of Silvanus).
- **Petilian:** The famous Donatist bishop who debated Augustine.
- **Constantine's City:** Renamed after the first Christian emperor.

### BAGAI (Khenchela)
**Coordinates:** 35.43°N, 7.14°E
**Significance:**
- **"The Holy City" of Donatism:** A fortress of the radical *Circumcellions*.
- **Battle:** Site of violent clashes between Imperial troops and Donatist fanatics.

### TIMGAD (Thamugadi)
**Coordinates:** 35.48°N, 6.46°E
**Significance:**
- **Optatus:** The "Gildonian" Donatist bishop who ruled Timgad like a prince and allied with the rebel Gildo.
- **Library:** Famous public library (archaeological ruins).

## 5. PEOPLES & POPULATIONS

### The Catholics (Nicenes)
- **Demographics:** Urban Romans, landowners, intellectuals.
- **Theology:** Universal church, validity of sacraments depends on Christ.
- **Leader:** Augustine, Aurelius of Carthage.

### The Donatists
- **Demographics:** Rural Berbers, peasants, "Punic" speakers.
- **Theology:** "Pure" church, sacraments invalid if priest is a sinner (*traditor*).
- **Identity:** Saw themselves as the true "Church of the Martyrs" resisting the "corrupt" State Church.

### The Circumcellions
- **Description:** Radical Donatist "shock troops." Roamed the countryside with clubs (called "Israels"), attacking Catholic clergy and freeing slaves.
- **Motto:** *Deo Laudes* ("Praise to God").
- **Social Banditry:** Often allied with peasant revolts against Roman landlords.

### The Vandals (Arians)
- **Role:** Germanic conquerors (429 AD).
- **Theology:** Denied the Trinity (Jesus is created).
- **Persecution:** Exiled hundreds of Catholic bishops (including Fulgentius) to Sardinia.

## 6. HISTORICAL SOURCES & EVIDENCE

### Primary Sources

**Augustine, *Confessions*:**
> "I came to Carthage, where a cauldron of unholy loves bubbled all around me..." (Describes his youth).
> "Late have I loved you, O Beauty so ancient and so new..." (Conversion).

**Acts of the Scillitan Martyrs (180 AD):**
> "We are citizens of heaven... we pay the tax... but we honor God alone." (Earliest Latin Christian text).

**Victor of Vita, *History of the Vandal Persecution*:**
> "They drove the bishops into the desert... they took the sacred vessels for their own feasts." (Eyewitness to Vandal atrocities).

**Tertullian, *Apology*:**
> "The blood of the martyrs is the seed of the church."

### Archaeological Evidence

**The "Memory" of the Martyrs:**
- **Mensae:** Stone tables found in basilicas used for feasts honoring martyrs.
- **Mosaic Tombs:** Tabarka mosaics showing the deceased in orante (praying) posture with candles.

**Donatist Churches:**
- **Identification:** Often inscribed with *Deo Laudes* (Donatist slogan) instead of *Deo Gratias* (Catholic).
- **Location:** High density in southern Numidia (Timgad region).

## 7. CONFLICTS & RESOLUTIONS

### Conflict 1: The Donatist Schism
**Debate:** Can a "traitor" bishop ordain a valid priest?
**Geography:** The map will show a "Heat Map" of Donatist strength.
- **Red Zone (Donatist):** Southern Numidia (Aurès Mountains).
- **Blue Zone (Catholic):** Proconsular Africa (Carthage/Coast).
**Resolution:** The Council of 411 (enforced by the State) broke the back of the Donatist hierarchy, but the schism lingered until the Islamic conquest.

### Conflict 2: The Disappearance of the Church
**Debate:** Why did the church survive in Egypt (Copts) but vanish in North Africa after Islam?
**YEC/Historical Analysis:**
- **Language:** The church remained Latin-speaking (colonial). It never translated the Bible into Berber (indigenous). When the Romans left, the faith had no "root" in the native tongue (unlike Coptic in Egypt).
- **Division:** Centuries of Donatist vs. Catholic infighting weakened the church spiritually and socially before the Arabs arrived.
- **Vandal Persecution:** The Arian Vandals decimated the Catholic clergy for 100 years, leaving the flock shepherdless.

## 10. PEDAGOGICAL NOTES

### Discussion Questions
1. **The Nature of the Church:** The Donatists wanted a "Pure Church" of saints. Augustine argued for a "Mixed Church" (wheat and tares). Which view is more biblical? (See Matthew 13).
2. **Church & State:** The Catholics used the power of the Roman State to suppress the Donatists. Was this a mistake? How did it set a precedent for the Middle Ages?
3. **The Missing Bible:** Why did the North African church fail to translate the Bible into Berber? How did this contribute to its eventual extinction under Islam? (Compare with the Coptic Bible in Egypt).
4. **Persecution:** How did the church survive the pagan Roman persecution (3rd century) but crumble under the Vandal (Arian) and Arab (Islamic) conquests?
5. **Canonization:** Why was it important for the North African councils to define the New Testament canon? What heresies (like Manichaeism) were they fighting?

### Classroom Activities
- **Council of Carthage Roleplay:** Divide the class into Catholics (Augustine) and Donatists (Petilian). Debate the question: "If a bishop sins, is the baptism he performs valid?"
- **Augustine's Map:** Trace Augustine's life on the map. Read excerpts from *Confessions* corresponding to each city (e.g., the "pear tree" incident in Thagaste, the "cauldron of loves" in Carthage).
- **Martyr's Mosaic:** Create a paper mosaic in the style of Tabarka, honoring a North African martyr like Perpetua.
- **Theology Chart:** Create a comparison chart: Donatism vs. Catholicism (View of Sin, View of Sacraments, View of Church).
