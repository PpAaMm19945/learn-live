# Map 6: Roman North Africa
## Africa Proconsularis, Numidia, Mauretania, Cyrenaica

---

## ERA & CHRONOLOGY

**Young Earth Dating:** c. 30 BC – 700 AD (Roman annexation through Islamic conquest)  
**Biblical Framework:**
- **Roman Period:** c. 30 BC – 476 AD (Fall of Western Rome)
- **Vandal Period:** c. 429–534 AD (Germanic invasion, Arian Christianity)
- **Byzantine Period:** c. 533–698 AD (Justinian reconquest)
- **Early Islamic Period:** c. 640–710 AD (Arab conquest)

**Conventional Equivalent:** Same dates (Late Antiquity, well-documented historical period)

**Key Dates:**
- **146 BC** — Destruction of Carthage, Africa Proconsularis established
- **46 BC** — Julius Caesar annexes Numidia
- **30 BC** — Augustus reorganizes North African provinces
- **429 AD** — Vandal invasion under Genseric
- **533 AD** — Byzantine reconquest under Belisarius
- **647–698 AD** — Islamic conquest (Egypt → Maghreb)

## BIBLICAL & CHURCH HISTORY FRAMEWORK

### New Testament Connections

**Acts 2:10** — Pentecost pilgrims from North Africa:
> "Phrygia and Pamphylia, Egypt and the parts of Libya belonging to Cyrene"

**Acts 6:9** — Cyrenian Jews in Jerusalem:
> "Synagogue of the Freedmen... Cyrenians, and Alexandrians"

**Acts 13:1** — Lucius of Cyrene at Antioch:
> "In the church at Antioch there were prophets and teachers... Lucius of Cyrene"

**Mark 15:21** — Simon of Cyrene carries Jesus' cross:
> "And they compelled a passerby, Simon of Cyrene, the father of Alexander and Rufus, to carry his cross"

### Early Church Fathers (North African)

**Tertullian** (c. 155–240 AD, Carthage):
- "Father of Latin Christianity"
- Wrote *Apologeticus* defending Christianity
- Coined theological terms (Trinity, sacrament)

**Cyprian of Carthage** (c. 200–258 AD):
- Bishop of Carthage, martyred under Valerian
- Wrote on church unity, baptism, martyrdom

**Augustine of Hippo** (354–430 AD):
- Bishop of Hippo Regius (modern Annaba, Algeria)
- *Confessions*, *City of God*, *On Christian Doctrine*
- Most influential Western theologian after Paul

**Athanasius of Alexandria** (c. 296–373 AD):
- Though Egyptian, his influence extended to North Africa
- Defender of Nicene orthodoxy against Arianism

### Church Councils

**Council of Carthage** (multiple councils, 3rd–5th centuries):
- 256 AD — Baptismal controversy (Cyprian vs. Rome)
- 397 AD — Confirmed NT canon (27 books)
- 418 AD — Condemned Pelagianism

**Donatist Controversy** (4th–5th centuries):
- North African schism over validity of sacraments from *traditores* (those who surrendered scriptures during persecution)
- Augustine debated Donatists, defended Catholic unity

## GEOGRAPHIC COVERAGE

### Map Boundaries
- **North:** Mediterranean Sea (37°N, Sicily/southern coast for context)
- **South:** Sahara Desert edge (~28°N, Atlas Mountains foothills)
- **East:** Cyrenaica/Egypt border (~25°E, Gulf of Sidra)
- **West:** Atlantic Ocean, Morocco coast (~10°W, Tangier/Tingis)

### Roman Provincial Divisions (c. 30 BC – 476 AD)

**1. Africa Proconsularis** (Tunisia, eastern Algeria):
- **Capital:** Carthage (rebuilt by Augustus)
- **Major Cities:** Utica, Hadrumetum (Sousse), Thysdrus (El Jem)
- **Economy:** Grain production ("breadbasket of Rome"), olive oil

**2. Numidia** (eastern Algeria):
- **Capital:** Cirta (Constantine)
- **Status:** Imperial province (3rd century AD)
- **Cities:** Hippo Regius, Thagaste (Augustine's birthplace), Thamugadi (Timgad)

**3. Mauretania Caesariensis** (western Algeria):
- **Capital:** Caesarea (Cherchell, named after Augustus)
- **Founded:** 42 AD (emperor Claudius)

**4. Mauretania Tingitana** (northern Morocco):
- **Capital:** Tingis (Tangier)
- **Extent:** Atlantic coast to Sala (Rabat), limited southern reach

**5. Cyrenaica** (eastern Libya):
- **Capital:** Cyrene
- **Status:** Combined with Crete as single province (67 BC)
- **Cities:** Apollonia (port), Berenice (Benghazi), Ptolemais, Taucheira

## MAJOR GEOGRAPHIC FEATURES

### Mediterranean Coast ("Tell" Region)
- **Length:** ~3,000 km (Cyrenaica to Atlantic)
- **Climate:** Mediterranean (wet winters, grain/olive agriculture)
- **Ports:** Carthage, Hippo Regius, Caesarea, Tingis, Leptis Magna, Cyrene

### Atlas Mountains
- **Extent:** Morocco, Algeria (parallel to coast)
- **Roman Control:** Coastal plains and valleys, limited mountain penetration
- **Berber Resistance:** Mountain tribes maintained autonomy (Gaetuli, Mauri)

### Sahara Desert (Northern Edge)
- **Roman Frontier:** *Limes Tripolitanus* (fortified border, 3rd century AD)
- **Garamantes Kingdom:** Fezzan region (indirect Roman contact, Cornelius Balbus expedition 19 BC)
- **Trans-Saharan Trade:** Limited Roman engagement (camels not yet widespread)

### Major Rivers (Seasonal Wadis)
- **Bagradas (Medjerda):** Tunisia, flows to Gulf of Tunis
- **Chelif:** Algeria, longest river
- Most North African rivers are seasonal (*wadis*), not navigable

## MAJOR CITIES & SITES

### CARTHAGE (Tunisia)
**Coordinates:** 36.85°N, 10.32°E  
**History:**
- Destroyed 146 BC (Third Punic War), rebuilt by Augustus (29 BC)
- Capital of Africa Proconsularis
- Population: ~500,000 (3rd century AD, rival to Rome)
- Major Christian center (Tertullian, Cyprian, Augustine councils)
- Vandal capital (439–534 AD)
- Byzantine capital (534–698 AD)

**Key Sites:**
- Antonine Baths (largest outside Rome)
- Amphitheater (martyrdom site)
- Christian basilicas (Damous el-Karita)

---

### HIPPO REGIUS (Annaba, Algeria)
**Coordinates:** 36.88°N, 7.86°E  
**Significance:**
- Augustine's bishopric (396–430 AD)
- Besieged by Vandals (430 AD, Augustine died during siege)
- Augustine's library/writings preserved

---

### CIRTA (Constantine, Algeria)
**Coordinates:** 36.37°N, 6.61°E  
**History:**
- Numidian capital (Massinissa, 3rd–2nd c. BC)
- Renamed Constantine (after Emperor Constantine, 4th c. AD)
- Strategic plateau location (defensive)

---

### LEPTIS MAGNA (Libya)
**Coordinates:** 32.64°N, 14.29°E  
**Significance:**
- Birthplace of Emperor Septimius Severus (r. 193–211 AD)
- Monumental architecture (forum, basilica, arch of Severus)
- Grain/oil export port

---

### TINGIS (Tangier, Morocco)
**Coordinates:** 35.76°N, 5.80°W  
**Significance:**
- Capital of Mauretania Tingitana

## PEOPLES & POPULATIONS

### Romans (Colonial Elite)
- **Military veterans** settled in colonies (Carthage, Timgad)
- **Administrative class** (governors, tax collectors)
- **Latin language** spread (North African Latin dialect)

### Romanized Berbers
- **Urban elites** (adopted Roman citizenship, Latin names)
- **Examples:** Septimius Severus (Berber-Punic origin), Augustine (Berber mother, Monica)

### Indigenous Berbers
- **Mountain tribes:** Gaetuli, Mauri (resisted Romanization)
- **Language:** Berber dialects (Afro-Asiatic, pre-Roman continuity)
- **Economy:** Pastoralism, transhumance (seasonal migration)

### Jewish Communities
- **Cyrenaica:** Large Jewish population (Acts 2:10, 6:9)
- **Carthage:** Jewish Quarter
- **Revolts:** 115–117 AD (Trajan), 132–135 AD (Bar Kokhba, spillover)

### Christian Communities (1st–7th centuries AD)
- **Early spread:** 1st century (Acts 2:10, North African pilgrims)
- **Martyrs:** Perpetua and Felicity (Carthage, 203 AD)
- **Donatist schism:** 4th–5th centuries (North African division)
- **Vandal persecution:** Arian Vandals vs. Catholic Christians (5th–6th c.)

### Vandals (Germanic Invaders, 429–534 AD)
- **Invasion:** 429 AD (Genseric, 80,000 people from Spain)
- **Kingdom:** Eastern Algeria, Tunisia, Tripolitania
- **Religion:** Arian Christianity (persecuted Catholics)
- **Conquest:** 534 AD (Byzantine general Belisarius)

## CONFLICTS & RESOLUTIONS

### Conflict 1: North African Christianity — Indigenous vs. Imported?

**Question:** Did Christianity spread naturally from apostolic times (Acts 2:10), or only via later Roman missionaries?

**Evidence:**
- **Acts 2:10** — Cyrenian Jews at Pentecost (33 AD), likely returned with Gospel
- **Acts 11:20** — "Men of Cyrene" preached in Antioch (c. 40 AD)
- **Tertullian** (c. 200 AD) — Implies well-established church by 2nd century

**YEC Resolution:**
- **Apostolic origin:** North African Christianity dates to 1st century (Acts 2:10, 11:20)
- **Cyrenian Jews → Christian mission:** Jewish diaspora became Christian evangelists
- **Indigenous spread:** Not dependent on Roman imperial Christianity (pre-Constantine)

---

### Conflict 2: Donatist Schism — Theological or Political?

**Context:** 4th–5th century North African schism over validity of sacraments from *traditores* (clergy who surrendered scriptures during Diocletian persecution, 303–305 AD)

**Donatist Position:**
- Sacraments invalid if performed by unworthy clergy
- Church must be pure (*ecclesia sancta*)

**Catholic Position (Augustine):**
- Sacraments valid *ex opere operato* (by the work performed), not dependent on clergy's holiness
- Church is mixed body (wheat and tares, Matthew 13:24-30)

**YEC Perspective:**
- **Biblical ecclesiology:** Augustine's view aligns with NT church (1 Corinthians 3:1-9, church contains immature/sinful members)
- **Donatism = sectarianism:** Similar to Montanism, Novatianism (over-emphasis on purity)

---

### Conflict 3: Vandal Arianism — Heresy or Cultural Christianity?

**Vandals:** Arian Christians (denied full deity of Christ, affirmed Jesus as created being)

**Catholic View:** Heresy (Council of Nicaea 325 AD condemned Arianism)

**Vandal Practice:**
- Persecuted Catholic Christians (exile, property confiscation)
- Closed Catholic churches, ordained Arian bishops

**YEC Resolution:**
- **Arianism = heresy:** Denies deity of Christ (John 1:1, Colossians 2:9)
- **Vandal persecution:** Martyrdom accounts (Victor of Vita, *History of the Vandal Persecution*)
- **Byzantine reconquest (534 AD):** Restored Catholic Christianity

---

### Conflict 4: Islamic Conquest — Rapid or Gradual?

**Conventional View:** North Africa Islamized rapidly (640–710 AD, 70 years)

**Problem:** How did deeply Christian region (Augustine's homeland) convert so quickly?

**YEC Analysis:**
1. **Byzantine weakness:** Monophysite controversy, heavy taxation, local resentment
2. **Berber resistance:** Indigenous Berbers (Kusaila, Queen Kahina) fought Arabs initially, then converted
3. **Islamic *jizya*:** Non-Muslims paid poll tax (incentive to convert)
4. **Gradual Islamization:** Conversion process took 200+ years (Christian communities persisted to 12th century)

**Resolution:**
- **Conquest ≠ conversion:** Military conquest (640–710 AD) ≠ full Islamization (took centuries)
- **Christian remnant:** Coptic Christianity survived in Egypt, small communities in Maghreb until medieval period

## PEDAGOGICAL NOTES

### Discussion Questions

1. **Biblical Geography:**
   - Why were so many early Christians from Cyrene (Acts 2:10, 6:9, 11:20, 13:1)? What role did the Jewish diaspora play in spreading the Gospel?
   - How does Simon of Cyrene carrying Jesus' cross (Mark 15:21) illustrate God's sovereignty in using North Africans in redemptive history?

2. **Church History:**
   - How did North African church fathers (Tertullian, Cyprian, Augustine) shape Western Christianity? What theological concepts did they develop?
   - What was the Donatist controversy? How did Augustine's response shape our understanding of the church and sacraments?

3. **Geography & Economy:**
   - Why was North Africa called the "breadbasket of Rome"? How did Roman control of grain supply affect the empire?
   - How did the Mediterranean climate and coastal plains make North Africa ideal for agriculture?

4. **Missions & Persecution:**
   - How did persecution under Rome (Perpetua, Cyprian) strengthen the North African church?
   - How did Vandal Arianism (429–534 AD) challenge Catholic Christianity? What does this teach about theological precision?

### Classroom Activities

1. **Map Labeling Exercise:**
   - Provide blank map, students label: provinces, capitals, Atlas Mountains, Mediterranean Sea
   - Identify cities mentioned in Acts (Cyrene) and church fathers' cities (Carthage, Hippo)

2. **Timeline Construction:**
   - Create visual timeline: Carthage destroyed (146 BC) → rebuilt (29 BC) → Tertullian (200 AD) → Augustine (400 AD) → Vandals (429 AD)

3. **Primary Source Reading:**
   - Read excerpts: Augustine's *Confessions* (conversion account), Tertullian's *Apologeticus* (defense of Christianity)
   - Discuss: How did North African context shape their theology?

4. **Comparative Study:**
   - Compare Roman North Africa map (Map 6) with earlier North Africa map (Map 4: Phut's descendants)
   - Trace continuity: Berber peoples, Phoenician/Carthaginian heritage, Roman conquest

5. **Grain Trade Simulation:**
   - Calculate: If Rome needed 400,000 tons of grain/year, and Africa Proconsularis supplied 2/3, how much grain shipped from Carthage? How many ships needed?
