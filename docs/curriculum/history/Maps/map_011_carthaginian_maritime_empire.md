# Map 11: Carthaginian Maritime Empire (c. 814-146 BC)
## The Phoenician West, Punic Wars, and the "Ships of Tarshish"

---

## 1. ERA & CHRONOLOGY

**Young Earth Dating:** c. 814 BC – 146 BC (Founding to Destruction)  
**Biblical Framework:**
- **Post-Babel Dispersion:** c. 2200 BC (Canaanite migration to Levant)
- **Phoenician Expansion:** c. 1200-800 BC (Post-Exodus/Judges period)
- **Carthaginian Hegemony:** c. 550-202 BC (Dominance of Western Med)
- **Destruction:** 146 BC (Prophetic fulfillment of judgment on Canaanite religion)

**Conventional Equivalent:** Same dates (Classical period is well-attested)

**Key Dates:**
- **c. 969 BC** — Hiram of Tyre assists Solomon (height of Phoenician power, 1 Kings 5)
- **814 BC** — Traditional founding of Carthage by Dido/Elissa (accepted in YEC)
- **c. 600 BC** — Carthage becomes independent of Tyre (Tyre falls to Babylon)
- **c. 550 BC** — Battle of Alalia; Carthage and Etruscans defeat Greeks, securing Corsica/Sardinia
- **c. 500 BC** — Voyage of Hanno the Navigator (West African coast)
- **480 BC** — Battle of Himera; Carthaginian defeat in Sicily (same year as Salamis)
- **264-241 BC** — **First Punic War:** Rome takes Sicily.
- **218-201 BC** — **Second Punic War:** Hannibal crosses Alps; Battle of Cannae; Battle of Zama.
- **149-146 BC** — **Third Punic War:** Scipio Aemilianus destroys Carthage; fields sown with salt (legend).

**Chronology Compression (YEC vs. Conventional):**
- **Phoenician Origins:** Conventional archaeology often dates "Canaanite" urbanization to 3000 BC. YEC compresses this to post-2200 BC (Post-Babel).
- **Iron Age Transition:** The collapse of Bronze Age powers (c. 1200 BC conventional) aligns with the chaotic Judges period, allowing Phoenician city-states to rise as maritime powers without imperial interference.
- **The "Dark Age" Gap:** Conventional history posits a "Dark Age" in Greece/Med (1200-800 BC). YEC views this as a stretched timeline; the actual gap is shorter, meaning Phoenician expansion happened rapidly after the Exodus/Conquest disrupted Canaan.

## 2. BIBLICAL & CHURCH HISTORY FRAMEWORK

### Old Testament Connections

**Genesis 10:15-19 — Origin of Phoenicians:**
> "Canaan begot Sidon his firstborn, and Heth... and the border of the Canaanites was from Sidon as you go toward Gerar..."
- **Identification:** Carthaginians (Punics) are displaced Canaanites. They called themselves *Chanani* (Canaanites). Augustine noted that Punic peasants in his day still identified as "Canaanites."

**Ezekiel 27 — The Wealth of Tyre:**
> "Tarshish was your merchant because of your many luxury goods. They gave you silver, iron, tin, and lead for your goods." (v. 12)
- **Tarshish Connection:** Likely Tartessos in Spain, reached via Carthage. The "Ships of Tarshish" were the heavy freighters of the ancient world.
- **Global Trade:** The chapter lists trade partners from "Javan" (Greece) to "Dedan" (Arabia), showing the vast network Carthage inherited.

**Isaiah 23 — The Burden Against Tyre:**
> "Wail, you ships of Tarshish! For it is laid waste... Cross over to Tarshish; wail, you inhabitants of the coastland!"
- **Prophecy:** Predicts the flight of Tyrians to their western colonies (Carthage/Spain) when Tyre is besieged (by Assyria/Babylon/Alexander).

**Jonah 1:3 — The Flight to Tarshish:**
> "But Jonah arose to flee to Tarshish from the presence of the LORD. He went down to Joppa, and found a ship going to Tarshish..."
- **Geography:** Jonah tried to go as far *west* as possible (Spain/Carthage) to escape a mission to the *east* (Nineveh).

### Theological Significance

**Judgment on Canaanite Religion:**
- Carthage preserved the fierce Canaanite religion (Baal Hammon, Tanit) long after it was crushed in the Levant.
- **Child Sacrifice (Tophet):** Jeremiah 19:5 condemns burning sons for Baal. Carthage practiced this on an industrial scale (thousands of urns found).
- **Divine Justice:** The total destruction of Carthage by Rome (146 BC) can be viewed as the final judgment on this persistent Canaanite abomination, similar to the conquest of Joshua. The "Curse of Canaan" (Genesis 9:25) plays out in their subjugation by Rome (Japheth).

**1. The Punic Heartland (Tunisia):**
- **Cap Bon Peninsula:** Fertile agricultural zone (Megara). Famous for Mago's treatise on agriculture.
- **Bagradas Valley:** Wheat fields feeding the empire.
- **Sahel:** Olive growing region to the south (Hadrumetum).

**2. The Western Islands:**
- **Sicily (West):** The main battleground with Greeks (Syracuse). Key cities: Panormus, Lilybaeum, Drepanum.
- **Sardinia:** Grain and mineral source. Nuragic culture under Punic domination.
- **Corsica:** Timber for the fleet. Alalia colony.
- **Balearic Islands:** Famous for their slingers (mercenaries).

**3. Iberia (Spain):**
- **Baetica (Andalusia):** The silver mines of Rio Tinto.
- **Gades (Cadiz):** Ancient Phoenician port, gateway to Atlantic.
- **The Barcid Empire:** Southeast Spain, conquered by Hamilcar Barca to pay war debts to Rome.

**4. The "Metropolis" (Levant Inset):**
- **Phoenicia:** Tyre, Sidon, Byblos. The mother cities.

## 4. MAJOR CITIES & SITES

### CARTHAGE (Qart-Hadasht)
**Coordinates:** 36.85°N, 10.32°E
**Significance:**
- **"New City":** Founded 814 BC.
- **The Cothon:** Famous circular naval harbor (held 220 warships) and rectangular commercial harbor.
- **Byrsa Hill:** The citadel/acropolis.
- **Tophet:** Sanctuary of Tanit where child sacrifice occurred (archaeological site *Salammbô*).
- **Walls:** Triple walls, 45 feet high, housing elephants and horses.

### UTICA
**Coordinates:** 37.05°N, 10.06°E
**Significance:**
- "Old City" (founded c. 1100 BC conventional).
- Rival to Carthage, often allied with Rome.
- Port silted up today (now inland).

### GADES (Cadiz, Spain)
**Coordinates:** 36.53°N, -6.29°W
**Significance:**
- Founded c. 1100 BC (traditional).
- Temple of Melqart (Hercules) - Hannibal prayed here before attacking Rome.
- Key trans-shipment point for British tin and Spanish silver.

### PANORMUS (Palermo, Sicily)
**Coordinates:** 38.11°N, 13.36°E
**Significance:**
- "All Port" (Greek name). Major Punic stronghold in Sicily.
- Base for fleets attacking Italy.

### NEW CARTHAGE (Cartagena, Spain)
**Coordinates:** 37.60°N, -0.98°W
**Significance:**
- Founded by Hasdrubal the Fair (228 BC).
- Base for Hannibal's campaign against Rome.
- Captured by Scipio Africanus (209 BC) in a daring low-tide assault.

### LIXUS (Larache, Morocco)
**Coordinates:** 35.20°N, -6.11°W
**Significance:**
- Atlantic outpost.
- Trade in gold, ivory, and exotic animals from Africa.

## 5. PEOPLES & POPULATIONS

### The Punic Elite (Canaanites)
- **Origin:** Levantine Phoenicians.
- **Language:** Punic (West Semitic, close to Hebrew).
- **Structure:** Oligarchic Republic (Suffetes = Judges).
- **Role:** Merchants, naval officers, plantation owners.
- **Religion:** Syncretistic but core Canaanite pantheon (Baal, Astarte/Tanit, Melqart).

### Libyans (Berbers)
- **Role:** The agricultural peasantry and foot soldiers.
- **Status:** Often oppressed; frequent revolts (e.g., Mercenary War).
- **Biblical:** Descendants of **Phut**.

### Numidians
- **Tribes:** Massylii and Masaesyli.
- **Role:** Allies/Mercenaries. Famous light cavalry.
- **King Massinissa:** Key figure in Carthage's fall.

### Iberians/Celtiberians
- **Location:** Spain.
- **Role:** Mercenaries (heavy infantry), silver miners.
- **Falcatas:** Famous curved swords used by Hannibal's troops.

## 6. HISTORICAL SOURCES & EVIDENCE

### Primary Sources

**Herodotus, *Histories* (c. 440 BC):**
> "The Carthaginians say that they trade with a race of men who live in a part of Libya beyond the Pillars of Hercules... they lay their goods on the beach... the natives come and lay gold beside the goods." (Silent Trade).

**Aristotle, *Politics*:**
- Praises the Carthaginian constitution as one of the best, balancing kings (suffetes), nobles, and commons.

**Polybius, *The Histories*:**
- Eyewitness to the destruction of Carthage (146 BC). Describes the city's layout and the final siege.
- "At the sight of the city utterly perishing... Scipio is said to have shed tears and wept openly... reflecting that the same fate must one day befall Rome."

**The Periplus of Hanno (c. 500 BC):**
- Describes a voyage down the West African coast, seeing "burning mountains" (Mt. Cameroon?) and "gorillas."

### Archaeological Evidence

**The Tophet (Carthage):**
- **Finds:** Thousands of urns containing charred bones of infants and animals.
- **Stelae:** Inscriptions dedicating the "offering" (*molk*) to Tanit and Baal Hammon.
- **Debate:** Some argue they were stillborns, but the correlation with crisis years and biblical parallels (Jeremiah 19) strongly supports sacrificial killing.

**The Marsala Ship (Sicily):**
- **Find:** Punic warship (liburnian/quinquereme) found off Sicily.
- **Construction:** Prefabricated parts with Punic letters (assembly instructions) painted on the hull—evidence of mass production.

**Rio Tinto Mines (Spain):**
- **Evidence:** Massive slag heaps dating to Phoenician/Roman times. Analysis shows silver and lead extraction on an industrial scale.

## 7. CONFLICTS & RESOLUTIONS

### Conflict 1: The Location of Tarshish
**Debate:** Is Tarshish in Spain (Tartessos), Turkey (Tarsus), or a poetic term for "open sea"?
**Biblical Evidence:**
- Jonah flees *west* from Joppa (Jonah 1:3).
- It is a source of silver, iron, tin, lead (Ezekiel 27:12)—metals abundant in Iberia, not Tarsus.
- 1 Kings 10:22 mentions "ships of Tarshish" bringing gold, silver, ivory, apes, and peacocks (likely a separate fleet operating in the Red Sea/Indian Ocean, but using the *class* of ship known as a "Tarshish-ship").
**Resolution:** Map will identify **Tartessos (Southern Spain)** as the biblical Tarshish, with Carthage as the gatekeeper of this trade route.

### Conflict 2: Child Sacrifice — Fact or Roman Propaganda?
**Debate:** Revisionists claim Romans invented the "baby killer" myth to justify genocide.
**Evidence:**
- Biblical texts (Jeremiah, Kings) confirm Canaanites practiced this centuries *before* Rome existed.
- Tophet archaeology shows healthy infants (not just neonates) and animal substitutes, consistent with sacrificial rites.
- Kleitos (Greek historian) and Tertullian (Church Father) both confirm the practice.
**YEC/Biblical View:** We accept the reality of this practice as a defining sin of Canaanite culture, leading to their divine judgment.

## 10. PEDAGOGICAL NOTES

### Discussion Questions
1. **Economic Warfare:** How did Carthage's monopoly on the "Pillars of Hercules" (Gibraltar) allow them to control the price of tin (essential for making bronze) and silver?
2. **Prophecy Fulfillment:** Read Isaiah 23. How does the "wailing of the ships of Tarshish" relate to the fall of Tyre and the shift of power to Carthage?
3. **The Punic Wars:** Why was the First Punic War fought primarily over Sicily? Look at the map—why is Sicily the "key" to the central Mediterranean?
4. **Cultural Persistence:** Why did the Canaanite religion (Baal worship) survive in Carthage for 1,000 years after Joshua's conquest of Canaan? What does this say about the "stubbornness" of pagan traditions?
5. **Geography & Strategy:** Hannibal chose to cross the Alps rather than the sea. Looking at the map, why? (Roman naval dominance after First Punic War).

### Classroom Activities
- **"Ships of Tarshish" Tycoon:** A trading game. Students must sail from Tyre to Gades, avoiding Roman patrols and storms, to collect silver and tin.
- **The Cothon Project:** Build a model or draw a diagram of Carthage's famous circular harbor. Calculate how many ships it could hold (archaeology says ~220).
- **Hannibal's Logistics:** Using the map's scale bar, calculate the distance Hannibal marched from New Carthage to Cannae. Discuss the logistical nightmare of moving elephants across the Alps.
- **Propaganda Poster:** Create a Roman propaganda poster ("Carthago Delenda Est") focusing on the child sacrifice (Tophet) to justify the war.
