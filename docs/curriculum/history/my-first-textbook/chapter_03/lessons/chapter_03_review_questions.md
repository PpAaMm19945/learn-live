# Chapter 3: Review Questions

1.  **Identify and Describe:** Who were the Garamantes, and what key technological innovation allowed them to build a kingdom in the Sahara desert?

2.  **Compare and Contrast:** Describe the primary differences between the Phoenician settlement of North Africa (e.g., Carthage) and the later Roman conquest of the same region. What were their different motivations and methods?

3.  **Historical Connection:** Explain the historical and biblical significance of the pharaoh Sheshonq I (also known as Shishak). Which two major people groups from this chapter does he connect?

4.  **Cause and Effect:** How did the end of the Punic Wars and the destruction of Carthage directly lead to both the "golden age" of the Numidian kingdom and its eventual downfall?

5.  **Historical-Theological Synthesis:** The story of Israel is one of God's covenant faithfulness to a specific chosen people. The story of the descendants of Phut is one of powerful, often pagan, kingdoms rising and falling. How do these two different stories work together to reveal a fuller picture of God's sovereign plan for all of human history?
