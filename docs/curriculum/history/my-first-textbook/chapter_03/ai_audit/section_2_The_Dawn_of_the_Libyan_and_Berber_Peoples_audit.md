## Suggested Tonal Adjustments

- **Use shorter sentences:** Break up complex thoughts into more digestible pieces.
- **Prefer active voice:** Make your sentences more direct and powerful.
- **Use contractions:** Words like "don't" and "we'll" make the tone more conversational.
- **Incorporate anecdotes:** Start sections with a brief story or question to engage the reader.
- **Vary sentence openings:** Avoid starting multiple sentences the same way.

### Example 1

**Original Text:**
```
As the climate of North Africa grew harsher and the Sahara expanded, the descendants of Phut were forged into distinct and resilient peoples. The shared hardship of a drying land did not scatter them into oblivion; instead, it concentrated them into identifiable tribal groups, each adapting to the unique geography of their new homes. It is during this era that these groups first step out of the shadows of prehistory and into the light of the historical record, thanks to their powerful and literate neighbors in Egypt. The Egyptians, ever vigilant of their western frontier, began to document the peoples they encountered there. Their inscriptions give us the earliest names for these western tribes: the **Tjehenu** and the **Tjemehu**. These are the ancient names for the people we now know as the Libyans, the ancestors of the modern Amazigh, or Berber, peoples.
```
**Suggested Rewrite:**
> Try converting from passive to active voice. For example, instead of saying "The decision was made by the council," say "The council made the decision."
