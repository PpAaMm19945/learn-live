## 7.9 Chronological Summary

| Date (conventional estimate) | Event                                                                     | Key Persons                      |
| ---------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------- |
| c. 100 BC – 300 AD           | Rise of Aksum as a trading polity                                         | Aksumite rulers                  |
| c. 320–330s AD               | Frumentius organizes Christian community; consecration linked to Alexandria | Frumentius (Abuna), Aedesius     |
| mid-4th c.                   | Reign and conversion of King Ezana (inscriptional evidence)               | King Ezana                       |
| early–mid 6th c.             | Kaleb’s expedition to Himyar (Yemen); Abraha’s prominence                 | King Kaleb (Ella Asbeha), Abraha |
| 5th–7th c.                   | Arrival/mission work of the “Nine Saints”; spread of monasticism          | Abba Aregawi et al.              |
| 7th c. onward                | Red Sea trade decline; Aksum’s political marginalization                  | —                                |
| c. 700–900 AD                | Highland consolidation and survival of Christian institutions             | Regional rulers, bishops         |
