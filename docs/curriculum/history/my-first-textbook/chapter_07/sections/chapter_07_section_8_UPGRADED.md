## 7.8 Communities at the Margins: The Beta Israel (c. 4th–9th centuries)

In this section you will learn about the "Beta Israel," the ancient Jewish community of Ethiopia. We'll explore their mysterious origins and their unique role in the religious landscape of the Horn of Africa.

### Quick Start
*   **Learning Focus:** Explore the origins and identity of the Beta Israel (Ethiopian Jews).
*   **Key Idea:** The presence of a Jewish community practicing a pre-Talmudic form of the faith suggests ancient connections to Israel, creating a religiously diverse society long before the arrival of Islam.

### The "House of Israel"
While Aksum was a Christian empire, it was never a monolith. In the mountains north of Lake Tana (the Gondar region), a distinct community maintained a Jewish identity for centuries. They called themselves **Beta Israel** ("House of Israel"), though neighbors often used the pejorative term *Falasha* ("exiles").

The origins of the Beta Israel are debated. Some traditions trace them to the tribe of Dan or the entourage of Menelik I. Others suggest they were indigenous Agaw peoples who adopted Judaism from South Arabian traders. What is certain is that their faith was **pre-Talmudic**. They possessed the *Orit* (the Octateuch in Ge'ez) and practiced animal sacrifice—a rite that mainstream Judaism abandoned after the destruction of the Temple in 70 AD. This suggests their community was separated from the wider Jewish world at a very ancient date, preserving a "time capsule" of Old Testament faith.

### Religious Plurality and Conflict
The relationship between the Christian state and the Beta Israel varied. At times, they lived in uneasy coexistence. At other times, especially in later centuries, there was intense conflict as Christian kings sought to enforce religious unity. This dynamic shows that the Horn of Africa has always been a region of religious plurality, where Christianity, Judaism, and traditional religions (and later Islam) interacted in complex ways.

### Theological Reflection: The Root and the Branch
The existence of the Beta Israel reminds Christians of the Jewish roots of their faith. The Ethiopian Church, with its circumcision, dietary laws, and reverence for the Ark, already had a "Hebraic" character. The presence of a living Jewish community alongside it served as a constant reminder that the God of the Church is the God of Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob. It challenges us to respect the mystery of God's covenant people and to approach history with humility.
