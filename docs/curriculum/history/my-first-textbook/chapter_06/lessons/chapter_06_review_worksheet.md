1.  What was Egypt's primary economic importance to the Roman Empire?
2.  What does the term *Homoousios* mean, and why was it so important at the Council of Nicaea?
3.  Name the two primary forms of early Christian monasticism that emerged in Egypt and identify their key figures.
4.  What was the "Era of the Martyrs" and why is it significant for the Coptic calendar?
5.  What is a *libellus* and what pastoral problem did it create for the church?

## Critical Thinking Essays (150-250 words each)

1.  Analyze the role of the city of Alexandria as an "intellectual crossroads." How did its unique cultural and philosophical environment both help and challenge the development of early Christian theology?
2.  Compare and contrast the "red martyrdom" of the great persecutions with the "white martyrdom" of the desert monks. In what ways was monasticism a continuation of the martyr spirit in a new era?
3.  Discuss the statement: "The schism after the Council of Chalcedon was as much about politics and culture as it was about theology." To what extent do you agree or disagree? Use specific examples from the chapter.
4.  Explain the concept of "providence" as it relates to the Roman annexation of Egypt. How can a historical event driven by imperial ambition be seen as part of God's plan for the spread of the Gospel? Discuss the theological opportunities and challenges this view presents.
