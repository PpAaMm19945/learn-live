
### Section 8.4: The Early Trailblazers

**Primary Source Spotlight: Nok Terracotta - Model Answers**

1.  **What can the existence of such detailed artwork tell us about the structure of Nok society?**
    *   *Model Answer:* The existence of detailed artwork like the Nok terracotta heads suggests a society that was not solely focused on subsistence. It indicates a level of social complexity where there was enough surplus food and labor to support specialized artisans and craftspeople who were not farmers. This implies a structured society with a degree of social stratification and a cultural or religious system that valued such artistic expression, possibly for ritual, ancestor veneration, or to signify the status of leaders.

2.  **The Nok people were also among the earliest iron-workers in West Africa. How might the development of art and technology be related?**
    *   *Model Answer:* The development of art and technology are often closely related. The same skills required for advanced pottery (controlling fire in a kiln to create terracotta) are foundational for metallurgy (controlling fire in a furnace to smelt iron). A society that mastered one was well-positioned to develop the other. In addition, the wealth generated from superior iron tools and weapons could have created a more stable and prosperous society, which in turn could afford to support a class of artists. The tools used to create the detailed terracotta sculptures may themselves have been made of iron, allowing for greater artistic precision.
