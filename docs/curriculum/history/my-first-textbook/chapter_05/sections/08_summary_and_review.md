## 5.8 Chronological Summary & Review

In this final section, you will find a summary timeline of key events and a set of review questions to help you synthesize the material from this chapter.

This chapter traced the dramatic rise and fall of the church in Roman North Africa. We began with the establishment of Roman rule, which created the stable conditions for Christianity to spread from its origins in Cyrene. We witnessed the church's growth, its violent collision with imperial authority during the age of martyrs, and its golden age of intellectual flourishing led by giants like Tertullian, Cyprian, and Augustine. We also examined the deep internal wounds from the Donatist schism, the trauma of the Vandal invasion, and the fragile Byzantine restoration. Finally, we watched as this once-great church faded in the face of internal decline and the advance of Islam.

### Review Questions

2. According to the book of Acts, what is the earliest connection between North Africa and the Christian message?
3. What was the central issue of conflict between the early Christians and the Roman authorities?
4. Who were the *traditores*, and how did they lead to the Donatist Schism?
5. Name two major written works by Augustine of Hippo and describe their basic themes.
6. What was the primary religious difference between the Vandal rulers and the majority of the North African Christians they conquered?

**Critical Essay Prompts:**
1. Compare and contrast the leadership styles and primary concerns of Cyprian of Carthage and Augustine of Hippo. How did the specific challenges they faced shape their ministries? (150-250 words)
2. The "blood of the martyrs is the seed of the Church." Based on the events in this chapter, was this statement by Tertullian accurate for the African church? Discuss both the positive and negative impacts of persecution. (150-250 words)
3. Analyze the various factors (internal and external) that contributed to the decline of the North African church between 500 and 700 AD. Which factor do you believe was the most significant, and why? (150-250 words)
4. How does the story of the Donatist schism illustrate the ongoing tension between doctrinal purity and pastoral grace in the life of the church? (150-250 words)

### Synthesis & Scripture
The story of ancient North Africa is interwoven with the biblical narrative. The region known to the Romans as Africa was associated in the Hebrew Scriptures with the descendants of Phut (Genesis 10:6), and later with powers like Egypt and Ethiopia. The presence of Cyrenian Jews at Pentecost (Acts 2:10) marks a pivotal moment where the gospel explicitly intersects with this land. Reflect on how the rise of a powerful, Latin-speaking church in a region once on the periphery of Israel's story fulfills the prophetic vision of a faith that transcends ethnic and geographic boundaries (Isaiah 49:6).
