# About This Textbook

## Educational Approach

*The History of Africa* is designed as a comprehensive secondary-level textbook integrating:

- **Detailed historical and archaeological evidence** from the latest scholarship
- **Biblical-historical worldview** rooted in the 1689 Reformed Baptist Confession
- **Young Earth Creationist (YEC) chronology** with transparent engagement of mainstream dating
- **Primary source engagement** through excerpts and analysis

## Target Audience

This textbook is designed for:

- **High school students** (grades 9–12)
- **Homeschool families** seeking a Christ-centered African history curriculum
- **Christian classical education programs**
- **Church education** and adult study groups
- **University-level introductory courses** in African Christianity

## Pedagogical Features

Each chapter includes:

- **"Think It Through"** boxes for critical reflection and discussion
- **Primary Source excerpts** from ancient texts, inscriptions, and patristic writings
- **Historical Context sidebars** providing background information
- **High-quality maps** and visual aids with detailed metadata
- **Review Questions** for assessment and further study
- **Mermaid diagrams** for genealogies and timelines

## Theological Framework

This textbook operates from a confessional Reformed Baptist perspective:

- **Scripture as the ultimate authority** for understanding history
- **God's sovereignty** over all nations and peoples (Acts 17:26)
- **Biblical genealogies** as reliable historical records
- **Archaeological evidence** as confirmatory of biblical narratives
- **The Table of Nations** (Genesis 10) as the foundation for understanding African peoples

## Chronological Approach

Dates are presented with:

- **YEC-adjusted dating** as the primary chronology
- **Conventional/secular dates** in parentheses for reference
- **Transparent discussion** of dating methodologies and their assumptions
- **Archaeological synchronization** where applicable

### Dating Conventions Used

| Period | YEC Dating | Conventional Dating |
|--------|------------|---------------------|
| Post-Babel Dispersion | c. 2250 BC | c. 3000+ BC |
| Early Egyptian Dynasties | c. 2100–1900 BC | c. 3100–2686 BC |
| Kingdom of Kush (Kerma) | c. 1900–1500 BC | c. 2500–1500 BC |
| Aksumite Kingdom | c. 100–940 AD | c. 100–940 AD |
