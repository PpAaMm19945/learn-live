# Research: The Significance of Cush

**1. Political and Military Power:**
- **The 25th Dynasty:** Cushite kings from Nubia conquered and ruled Egypt from about 744 to 656 BCE. They didn't act like barbarian invaders. Instead, they saw themselves as restorers of traditional Egyptian values, reunifying the Nile Valley.
- **Notable Rulers:** Piye, Shabaka, Taharqa. The Bible mentions Taharqa (2 Kings 19:9, Isaiah 37:9) as a powerful king who challenged the Assyrian empire.

**2. Economic Clout:**
- **Trade Hub:** Cush was a major trade center. It connected sub-Saharan Africa with Egypt and the Mediterranean.
- **Valuable Resources:** Its key exports were gold, ivory, ebony, and incense.
- **Prophetic Mention:** Isaiah 45:14 ("The wealth of Egypt and the merchandise of Cush...") points to Cush's well-known economic strength. The verse shows Cush was a wealthy, known player on the world stage.

**3. Biblical and Prophetic Role:**
- **An Example of God's Universal Reach:**
    - **Amos 9:7:** God asks, "'Are you not like the Cushites to me, O people of Israel?' declares the LORD." This question puts Israel and Cush on equal footing. It challenges Israel's pride and shows God cares for all nations.
    - **Zephaniah 3:10:** "From beyond the rivers of Cush my worshipers, my scattered people, will bring me offerings." This prophecy looks to a time when people from the ends of the earth (symbolized by Cush) will come to worship God.
- **An Example of Inclusion:**
    - **Moses' Cushite Wife (Numbers 12:1):** The Bible explicitly states that Moses married a Cushite woman. When Miriam and Aaron challenged Moses over it, God rebuked them, not Moses. This story is a strong rebuke to racism within God's people.
    - **The Cushite Runner (2 Samuel 18:21-32):** At a key moment, Joab trusts a Cushite runner with the sensitive news of Absalom's death, bypassing an Israelite. The Bible depicts the runner as respectful, diplomatic, and trustworthy.
    - **The Mixed Multitude (Exodus 12:38):** A diverse group, including Egyptians and other Africans, left Egypt with the Israelites. They became part of God's foundational community, showing that God's people were multi-ethnic from the start.
