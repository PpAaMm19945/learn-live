# Lesson: Sola Scriptura and the Curse of Ham

**Concept:** Sola Scriptura (Scripture Alone)
- Definition: The Protestant Reformation principle that the Bible alone is the final authority for our faith and life.
- Application: We use this principle to challenge traditions and interpretations that people have added to Scripture over time.

**Case Study:** The "Curse of Ham"
- Genesis 9:20-27: Noah curses Canaan, Ham's son, not Ham himself.
- Historical Misinterpretation: Later, people wrongly applied the curse to all of Ham's descendants, especially those in Africa. They used this false idea to justify slavery and racial hierarchy. This is an example of "historical fiction."
- Sola Scriptura Analysis: A direct reading of the text shows the curse is specific to Canaan and his line. The Bible gives no basis for a racial curse on all Africans. In fact, God blessed Ham's other sons—Cush and Mizraim (Egypt)—who went on to found powerful civilizations.
