This chapter, "The Sovereign Hand," lays a theological and historical foundation for the study of African history from a biblical worldview. It argues that a true understanding of Africa's past must begin not with secular theories, but with the scriptural narrative of Creation, Fall, and Redemption. The chapter establishes that God is the primary actor in history, and His Word is the ultimate authority for interpreting all events.

The core of the chapter focuses on Genesis 10, the "Charter of Nations," identifying the descendants of Noah's son Ham—specifically Cush, Mizraim, and Phut—as the founders of Africa's earliest great civilizations. This positions Africa at the center of God's post-flood plan for humanity, affirming the inherent dignity and purpose of its peoples.

A significant portion of the chapter is dedicated to deconstructing the "Curse of Ham," a harmful myth used for centuries to justify slavery. Through careful exegesis of Genesis 9, the chapter demonstrates that the curse was specifically on Canaan, not Ham, and had nothing to do with race or skin color. The myth is exposed as a historical fiction created to legitimize an economic system based on greed.

The chapter also introduces key pedagogical and methodological frameworks. It establishes a Ussher-style biblical chronology as the primary timeline, while acknowledging conventional dates for reference. It defines key theological terms like sovereignty, covenant, and common grace, which are essential for a biblical analysis of history.

Finally, the chapter contrasts the biblical model of Africa's peopling with the secular Bantu Expansion theory. It calls on the reader to recognize the foundational worldview assumptions behind each model and to stand firm on the authority of Scripture. By doing so, the chapter equips students to engage with historical studies critically, with a framework that honors God and affirms the biblical narrative. The primary learning outcome is for students to see African history as an integral part of God's sovereign, redemptive plan for the world.
