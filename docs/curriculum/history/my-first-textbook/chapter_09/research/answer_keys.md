## Chapter 9: Ethiopia Alone: The Hidden Kingdom (c. 900–1500 AD)

### Section 9.3: The Solomonic Restoration (1270 AD)

**Primary Source Spotlight: The Kebra Nagast**

**1. According to this excerpt, what is the divine promise made to Menelik regarding the Ark of the Covenant? How does this promise legitimize his new kingdom in Ethiopia?**
*Model Answer:* The excerpt from the *Kebra Nagast* contains a divine promise that the Ark of the Covenant will "not be separated from thee for ever" and will accompany Menelik into the land of Ethiopia. This promise legitimizes his new kingdom by framing it as the chosen dwelling place of God's own presence, as symbolized by the Ark. The blessing of the city upon the Ark's entry signifies a direct transfer of divine favor and covenant from the old Zion (Jerusalem) to the new Zion (Ethiopia), making Menelik's kingdom not just a political entity, but a sacred one.

**2. What does the language used in this passage suggest about the relationship between political power and divine favor in the Ethiopian worldview?**
*Model Answer:* The language suggests that political power and divine favor are inextricably linked. The promise is made directly to Menelik ("thee and for thy seed for ever"), tying the legitimacy of the monarchy directly to the presence of the Ark. The Ark is a sign of God's approval, and its possession is the ultimate validation of the king's right to rule. This implies a worldview in which the state is a theological project and the monarch is a sacred figure, whose authority is derived not from the consent of the governed, but from a divine mandate to be the guardian of the covenant.
