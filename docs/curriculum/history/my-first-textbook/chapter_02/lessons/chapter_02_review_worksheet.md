## Part 2: Critical-Thinking Prompts

1.  **The Egyptian concept of *Ma'at* (divine order, harmony, and truth) was central to their civilization, while *Isfet* represented chaos. How did the Pharaoh's role relate to *Ma'at*, and what happened to the nation's stability and faith when *Ma'at* appeared to fail during the First Intermediate Period?**

2.  **Compare and contrast the Egyptian concept of "divine kingship" with the biblical model for a king as outlined in Deuteronomy 17. Discuss at least two major differences in their source of authority and their accountability.**

3.  **The prophets Isaiah and Ezekiel described Egypt in its Late Period as a "broken reed." Based on the historical events of this period (Kushite, Assyrian, and Persian rule), explain why this metaphor was so fitting. How did this contrast with Egypt's self-image from the New Kingdom?**

4.  **The story of Joseph is thought to fit best in the Hyksos period, and the Septuagint was translated during the Hellenistic period. Explain how God used these two distinct eras of foreign influence in Egypt to sovereignly advance His redemptive plan for Israel and the world.**
