## Suggested Tonal Adjustments

- **Use shorter sentences:** Break up complex thoughts into more digestible pieces.
- **Prefer active voice:** Make your sentences more direct and powerful.
- **Use contractions:** Words like "don't" and "we'll" make the tone more conversational.
- **Incorporate anecdotes:** Start sections with a brief story or question to engage the reader.
- **Vary sentence openings:** Avoid starting multiple sentences the same way.

### Example 1

**Original Text:**
```

The ruler of Egypt was not merely a king; he was considered a god on earth, a divine being whose role was to be the living bridge between the heavens and humanity. He was the earthly embodiment of the god Horus, and his primary duty was to maintain **Ma’at** (cosmic order) throughout the land. This belief in divine kingship became the central institution of the Egyptian state, concentrating immense religious and political power in one person. The Pharaoh was the guarantor of the Nile’s flood, the protector of the people from chaos (*Isfet*), and the ultimate authority on all matters spiritual and temporal.
```
**Suggested Rewrite:**
> Try converting from passive to active voice. For example, instead of saying "The decision was made by the council," say "The council made the decision."
