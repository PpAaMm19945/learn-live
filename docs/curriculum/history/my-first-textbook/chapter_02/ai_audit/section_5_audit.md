## Suggested Tonal Adjustments

- **Use shorter sentences:** Break up complex thoughts into more digestible pieces.
- **Prefer active voice:** Make your sentences more direct and powerful.
- **Use contractions:** Words like "don't" and "we'll" make the tone more conversational.
- **Incorporate anecdotes:** Start sections with a brief story or question to engage the reader.
- **Vary sentence openings:** Avoid starting multiple sentences the same way.

### Example 1

**Original Text:**
```

The biblical narrative in Genesis describes how **Joseph**, a Hebrew, rose from being a slave to becoming the vizier, the second-most powerful man in Egypt (Genesis 41). He accomplished this through his God-given wisdom to interpret Pharaoh’s dreams and administer a plan to save the nation from a devastating famine. Many scholars suggest that this story fits most comfortably within the Hyksos era. A foreign, Semitic ruler (a Hyksos king) would have been far more likely to elevate another Semite (Joseph) to such a high position than a native Egyptian Pharaoh would have been. This context makes Joseph's incredible rise to power historically plausible. It shows God’s providence in using a period of political disruption in Egypt to create a place of refuge for His covenant people, the family of Jacob, who were invited to settle in the fertile land of Goshen in the delta—the very heart of Hyksos-controlled territory.
```
**Suggested Rewrite:**
> Try converting from passive to active voice. For example, instead of saying "The decision was made by the council," say "The council made the decision."
