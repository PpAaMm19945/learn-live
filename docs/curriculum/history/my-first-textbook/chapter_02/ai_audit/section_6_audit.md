## Suggested Tonal Adjustments

- **Use shorter sentences:** Break up complex thoughts into more digestible pieces.
- **Prefer active voice:** Make your sentences more direct and powerful.
- **Use contractions:** Words like "don't" and "we'll" make the tone more conversational.
- **Incorporate anecdotes:** Start sections with a brief story or question to engage the reader.
- **Vary sentence openings:** Avoid starting multiple sentences the same way.

### Example 1

**Original Text:**
```

The Theban king **Ahmose I** successfully expelled the Hyksos around 1550 BC, reuniting the country and launching the New Kingdom, the most powerful and glorious era in Egyptian history. The humiliation of foreign rule had taught the Egyptians a harsh lesson: to prevent future invasions, they needed to build an empire. No longer content to stay within their traditional borders, the pharaohs of the New Kingdom became warrior-kings, leading a professional army equipped with the very chariots and advanced weapons the Hyksos had introduced. They were determined to project their power far beyond the Nile Valley.
```
**Suggested Rewrite:**
> Try converting from passive to active voice. For example, instead of saying "The decision was made by the council," say "The council made the decision."
