## Suggested Tonal Adjustments

- **Use shorter sentences:** Break up complex thoughts into more digestible pieces.
- **Prefer active voice:** Make your sentences more direct and powerful.
- **Use contractions:** Words like "don't" and "we'll" make the tone more conversational.
- **Incorporate anecdotes:** Start sections with a brief story or question to engage the reader.
- **Vary sentence openings:** Avoid starting multiple sentences the same way.

## Concrete Rewrite Examples

### Example 1

**Original Text:**
```
As the Pharaoh's power waned, regional governors, known as nomarchs, grew bolder. They had been entrusted with great authority to manage their provinces, and now they began to rule their territories as independent lords. Egypt, once a proud, unified nation, fractured into a collection of competing provinces. The flow of taxes to the capital at Memphis slowed to a trickle, and the grand, nation-unifying projects like pyramid building ceased. Brother fought against brother, and city fought against city. This period, known as the First Intermediate Period, was a time of intense chaos and civil war, a stark and terrifying manifestation of *Isfet* (chaos) overcoming the order that had defined their world for half a millennium.
```
**Suggested Rewrite:**
> Try converting from passive to active voice. For example, instead of saying "The decision was made by the council," say "The council made the decision."
