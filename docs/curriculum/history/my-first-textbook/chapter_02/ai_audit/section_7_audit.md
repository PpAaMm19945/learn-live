## Suggested Tonal Adjustments

- **Use shorter sentences:** Break up complex thoughts into more digestible pieces.
- **Prefer active voice:** Make your sentences more direct and powerful.
- **Use contractions:** Words like "don't" and "we'll" make the tone more conversational.
- **Incorporate anecdotes:** Start sections with a brief story or question to engage the reader.
- **Vary sentence openings:** Avoid starting multiple sentences the same way.

It was no longer the supreme, untouchable superpower of the Exodus. Instead, the prophets of Israel, like Isaiah and Jeremiah, spoke of Egypt as a flawed and unreliable political power. They repeatedly warned the kings of Judah not to make alliances with Egypt or to trust in its military might for protection against Assyria and Babylon. The prophet Ezekiel famously described Pharaoh as a "great monster" or "dragon" whose pride would be his downfall, and he labeled Egypt a “staff of reed” that would pierce the hand of anyone who leaned on it for support (Ezekiel 29:3-7). The kingdom that had built its identity on strength and permanence had become a symbol of false hope and inevitable decay, its fate now just one part of the larger political landscape being directed by the sovereign hand of God.

**Suggested Rewrite:**
> Try converting from passive to active voice. For example, instead of saying "The decision was made by the council," say "The council made the decision."
