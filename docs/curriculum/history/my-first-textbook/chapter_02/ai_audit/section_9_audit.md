## Suggested Tonal Adjustments

- **Use shorter sentences:** Break up complex thoughts into more digestible pieces.
- **Prefer active voice:** Make your sentences more direct and powerful.
- **Use contractions:** Words like "don't" and "we'll" make the tone more conversational.
- **Incorporate anecdotes:** Start sections with a brief story or question to engage the reader.
- **Vary sentence openings:** Avoid starting multiple sentences the same way.

## Concrete Rewrite Examples

### Example 1

**Original Text:**
```
With the death of Cleopatra, the grand story of ancient Egypt as an independent, sovereign power came to an end. Egypt was formally annexed by Rome and became the personal province of the Emperor Augustus. Its new role was clear and pragmatic: it was to be the **breadbasket of Rome**, its immense agricultural wealth harnessed to feed the empire’s capital. The Romans admired Egypt’s ancient culture and marveled at its monuments, but they did not see the land as a theological peer or a political rival. It was a resource to be managed, its divine kingship replaced by the secular, administrative power of Roman governors.
```
**Suggested Rewrite:**
> Try converting from passive to active voice. For example, instead of saying "The decision was made by the council," say "The council made the decision."
