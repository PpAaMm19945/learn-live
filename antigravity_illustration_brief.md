## 2. Art Style Specification: "Warm Codex"

### The Look
Think **Golden Age children's Bible illustration meets African textile warmth.** Not Pixar. Not flat vector. Not generic AI art.

**Reference artists/styles to channel:**
- **Jerry Pinkney** — rich watercolor textures, warm earth tones, dignified depiction of African peoples
- **E.B. Lewis** — luminous watercolor with strong compositional storytelling
- **Leo & Diane Dillon** — decorative borders, pattern-rich compositions, cultural specificity
- **Ethiopian manuscript illumination** — bold outlines, flat color fields, frontal figures, gold/ochre palette

### Color Palette (Tied to Brand)
All illustrations must feel cohesive with the app's existing palette:

| Token | Hex | Use in Illustrations |
|-------|-----|---------------------|
| Papyrus | `#F5ECD7` | Sky backgrounds, light areas, parchment textures |
| Night Ink | `#1A1A2E` | Outlines, deep shadows, night skies |
| Laterite | `#C4622D` | Earth, clay buildings, warm skin undertones |
| Amber | `#E8A838` | Sunlight, golden hour, divine light, wheat fields |
| Forest | `#2C6E49` | Vegetation, garden scenes, river banks |

### Style Rules (Non-Negotiable)
1. **People are depicted with dignity.** Dark skin tones rendered with warmth and beauty — rich brown, deep umber, warm sienna. Never grey, never washed out.
2. **Ancient Near Eastern / African clothing.** Simple draped linen, leather sandals, woven textiles with geometric patterns. No anachronisms.
3. **Landscape is specific.** Mesopotamian plains (flat, golden, ziggurats in distance) for Babel scenes. Nile valley (lush green strips flanking river, papyrus reeds) for Egypt. Savanna/highlands for Nubia. Arid golden coast for Libya.
4. **Architecture references real structures.** Ziggurats (stepped, mudbrick) for Babel. Early Egyptian mudbrick settlements for Mizraim. Nubian round huts with thatched roofs for Cush.
5. **Light source is warm and consistent.** Golden-hour lighting in most scenes. "God creating" scenes use cooler blue-to-gold gradient suggesting dawn.
6. **Borders/frames.** Each illustration should have a subtle decorative border — inspired by Ethiopian manuscript illumination or Kente cloth geometric patterns. The border should be thin (5-8% of frame width), consistent across all images in a band, and use the Laterite/Amber palette.
7. **No text in images.** The caption overlay is handled by the app. Images must be text-free.
8. **Faces are expressive but simple.** Not photorealistic. Warm, illustrative, with clear emotional expression readable by a 4-year-old. Think storybook, not portrait.

## 3. Scene-by-Scene Specifications

### BAND 0 (Ages 3–5) — 8 Images

Band 0 is a **picture book.** Images are full-screen with large, simple compositions. One clear focal point per image. Minimal background detail. Wide, open compositions a toddler can parse.

---

#### `band0_page01.jpg` — "God Creating the World"
**Caption context:** "Long, long ago, God made everything. He made the bright sun, the tall trees, and the deep blue water."

**Composition:** Cosmic/landscape scene. NO human figure for God — depict creation itself as the subject.
- **Center:** A brilliant golden sun rising over a new landscape
- **Left third:** Tall acacia trees with green canopy, silhouetted
- **Bottom third:** Deep blue water (ocean or river) reflecting the golden light
- **Sky:** Gradient from deep indigo-blue (top) through amber to warm gold at horizon
- **Mood:** Awe, beauty, dawn of everything. Luminous, warm.
- **Details:** Subtle birds in flight. A few flowers in foreground. Stars still faintly visible at the top of the sky (day breaking through night).

---

#### `band0_page02.jpg` — "Adam and Eve in the Garden"
**Caption context:** "God made the first people, Adam and Eve. He loved them very much and gave them a beautiful garden to live in."

**Composition:** Two figures (man and woman) standing together in a lush garden.
- **Figures:** Dark-skinned man and woman, simple draped clothing (or tasteful Eden depiction — leaves/natural coverings). Standing close, expressions of wonder and joy. Looking outward at the viewer or at the garden around them.
- **Setting:** Abundant garden — large tropical leaves, bright flowers (hibiscus, birds-of-paradise), fruit trees (figs, pomegranates). A stream or small waterfall in background.
- **Palette:** Dominated by Forest green and Amber. Dappled golden sunlight filtering through canopy.
- **Mood:** Peace, innocence, abundance.
- **Important:** Figures should be medium-sized in frame (visible head-to-knee). Not tiny. A child needs to clearly see "two people."

---

#### `band0_page03.jpg` — "People Building the Tower of Babel"
**Caption context:** "Many years passed, and there were lots of people. They forgot about God and wanted to build a very tall tower."

**Composition:** A massive stepped ziggurat under construction, with many small figures working.
- **Tower:** Center of frame, rising dramatically. Made of tan/brown mudbrick. Multiple stepped levels visible. Wooden scaffolding and ramps along the sides. The top is unfinished — actively being built.
- **People:** 15-20 small figures on ramps and at the base. Carrying bricks, pulling ropes, climbing. Diverse dark-skinned workers.
- **Setting:** Flat Mesopotamian plain. Golden-brown earth. Clear blue sky. The tower dominates the landscape.
- **Palette:** Laterite (mudbrick), Amber (dust/sunlight), Papyrus (sky).
- **Mood:** Ambition, busyness, human striving. Impressive but subtly hubristic — the tower looks too tall, almost unstable.

---

#### `band0_page04.jpg` — "People Confused at Babel"
**Caption context:** "God mixed up their words so they couldn't understand each other anymore! This place was called Babel."

**Composition:** Close-up of a group of 6-8 people at the base of the tower, looking confused and distressed.
- **Figures:** Various expressions of confusion — hands gesturing, mouths open (speaking different things), some looking bewildered, some frustrated, some covering ears. One person pointing one way, another pointing the opposite.
- **Background:** The tower partially visible behind them (lower portion), slightly out of focus. Construction has visibly stopped — abandoned tools, a toppled basket of bricks.
- **Palette:** Slightly cooler than the building scene — more muted amber, some blue-grey shadows suggesting the mood has shifted.
- **Mood:** Confusion, disruption, something has fundamentally changed.
- **Important:** Facial expressions must be VERY clear and readable for a 3-year-old. Exaggerated "confused" body language.

---

#### `band0_page05.jpg` — "Families Traveling Away from Babel"
**Caption context:** "The families had to stop building. They packed their things and moved away to find new homes."

**Composition:** Wide landscape scene showing multiple family groups walking away from a distant tower.
- **Foreground:** One family group close to viewer — a father figure leading, mother carrying a child on hip, an older child walking beside, a donkey loaded with bundles/baskets. They walk toward the left edge of the frame.
- **Middle ground:** Two more family groups visible, walking in different directions (right, and toward background).
- **Background:** The Tower of Babel small in the distance, center-right. Golden plain extending to horizon.
- **Paths:** The single road from the tower splits into 3 diverging paths — visual metaphor for the scattering.
- **Palette:** Warm Amber light. Long shadows suggesting late afternoon (the end of an era).
- **Mood:** Bittersweet journey. New beginnings. Families sticking together even as the world changes.

---

#### `band0_page06.jpg` — "Mizraim Traveling to Egypt"
**Caption context:** "One family leader was named Mizraim. He settled in a beautiful land with a big river. We call this land Egypt today."

**Composition:** A dignified man (Mizraim) arriving at the Nile river valley.
- **Figure:** Mizraim in foreground-right, standing tall, looking out at the landscape before him with one hand shielding his eyes from the sun. He wears a simple woven cloak over one shoulder, leather sandals, and carries a wooden staff. Behind him: his family (wife, 2-3 children, a few followers) and a loaded donkey.
- **Landscape:** The Nile valley opening before them. Wide blue river curving through lush green banks. Papyrus reeds at water's edge. Flat farmland on either side. Desert cliffs visible in far distance.
- **Palette:** Strong contrast between golden desert (behind Mizraim, where he came from) and the lush green/blue (where he's going). The Nile is blue with Amber reflections.
- **Mood:** Discovery, promise, "this is the place."
- **Reference geography:** Looking north from Upper Egypt. The valley is clearly life-giving in contrast to the surrounding arid land.

#### `band0_page07.jpg` — "Cush Traveling to Nubia"
**Caption context:** "Another family leader was named Cush. He moved his family to a land just south of Egypt, a place called Nubia."

**Composition:** Cush and his family arriving in the Nubian highlands/savanna.
- **Figure:** Cush in foreground-center, similar dignified pose to Mizraim but different appearance — perhaps taller, different cloak pattern. His family group slightly larger.
- **Landscape:** More rugged than Egypt. Rocky hills with scattered acacia trees. A tributary of the Nile visible (narrower, faster-moving water). Reddish-brown earth. Green grass on hillsides after rain.
- **Distinctive elements:** A few round huts with thatched roofs visible in middle distance (early settlement). Cattle grazing (Nubia was pastoral).
- **Palette:** More Laterite (red earth), Forest green (grass/trees), warm Amber sky.
- **Mood:** Strength, resilience, a different but equally beautiful homeland.

---

#### `band0_page08.jpg` — "Phut Traveling to Libya"
**Caption context:** "A third leader was named Phut. He took his family to a wide, sunny place called Libya."

**Composition:** Phut and family arriving at a bright, arid Mediterranean-adjacent landscape.
- **Figure:** Phut leading his family group toward the left of frame. Lighter clothing (white/cream linen — it's hot). Staff in hand.
- **Landscape:** Wide open golden plains. Bright, expansive sky (big sky country). Mediterranean sea visible as a blue strip on the horizon. Scattered date palms. Rocky outcroppings.
- **Distinctive elements:** The sense of vast, open space. More sky than land in the composition. A few goats with the family (pastoral).
- **Palette:** Brightest of the three settlement scenes — lots of Papyrus cream, intense Amber sunlight, touches of Mediterranean blue.
- **Mood:** Wide-open possibility, warmth, sun-drenched. "God loved all these families in their new homes."

### BAND 1 (Ages 6–7) — 15 Images

Band 1 is **Storybook+.** Same narrative but more detail in both text and image. Compositions can be slightly more complex than Band 0. More environmental detail, secondary elements, visual storytelling layers. Still clear and storybook-friendly, but a 7-year-old can handle more visual information.

**KEY DIFFERENCE FROM BAND 0:** Band 1 images should have:
- More background detail (architectural, environmental)
- More people in crowd scenes
- Slightly more sophisticated compositions (diagonals, depth)
- Subtle educational details embedded (e.g., correct architectural styles, geographic features)

#### `band1_page06.jpg` — "Table of Nations Map"
**Caption context:** "This great scattering of families is called the 'Table of Nations.' It tells us where different groups traveled."

**Composition:** An illustrated/artistic map — NOT a realistic landscape, but a STORY MAP.
- **Style:** Like a medieval mappa mundi or illustrated children's atlas. Bird's eye view.
- **Center:** Mesopotamia/Babel region with a small tower icon.
- **Radiating outward:** Illustrated paths with tiny caravan figures walking along them. Each path leads to a labeled region:
  - Southwest → **"Egypt"** (tiny pyramids icon — yes, anachronistic, but recognizable for kids)
  - South → **"Nubia"** (green highlands icon)
  - West → **"Libya"** (golden plains, palm tree icon)
  - Other directions (north, east) with smaller unlabeled paths to show global scope
- **Water bodies:** Mediterranean Sea, Red Sea, Nile River — simplified but geographically correct.
- **Border:** More elaborate decorative border for this image — it's a "document" within the story.
- **Palette:** Papyrus background (parchment feel), Laterite for land, blue for water, Amber for paths.

**IMPORTANT NOTE:** This is the ONE image where minimal text labels ARE acceptable ("Egypt," "Nubia," "Libya," "Babel"). Use a handwritten/manuscript-style font. These labels are part of the illustration, not UI text.

## 5. Execution Strategy

### Batch Order
Generate in this order to establish and refine style progressively:

**Batch 1 — Style Lock (3 images)**
1. `band0_page01.jpg` (Creation — establishes palette and texture)
2. `band0_page06.jpg` (Mizraim at the Nile — establishes character style and landscape)
3. `band1_page06.jpg` (Table of Nations map — establishes the illustrated-map style)

→ **STOP. Review these 3 for style consistency before proceeding.**

**Batch 2 — Band 0 Core (5 images)**
4. `band0_page02.jpg` through `band0_page05.jpg`, `band0_page07.jpg`, `band0_page08.jpg`

→ **Review Band 0 as a complete set.**

**Batch 3 — Band 1 Narrative (8 images)**
5. `band1_page01.jpg` through `band1_page05.jpg`, `band1_page07.jpg` through `band1_page11.jpg`

**Batch 4 — Band 1 Review Pages (4 images)**
6. `band1_page12.jpg` through `band1_page15.jpg`

### Prompt Engineering Notes
- **Always include in every prompt:** "Warm watercolor illustration style, children's storybook, African peoples with dark brown skin depicted with dignity, warm golden-hour lighting, decorative geometric border, no text in image"
- **Always exclude:** "cartoon, anime, 3D render, photorealistic, stock photo, clip art, AI-generated look, grey skin, European features"
- **Style anchor phrase:** "In the style of Jerry Pinkney's children's book illustrations with Ethiopian manuscript illumination borders"

### Iteration Protocol
- If a generated image has wrong skin tones → regenerate (non-negotiable)
- If composition is good but details are wrong → use edit/inpaint if available
- If style drifts between images → re-anchor with the style lock images from Batch 1
- Maximum 3 regeneration attempts per image before adjusting the prompt

## 7. What Success Looks Like

A parent opens the app, taps Chapter 1, and their 4-year-old sees illustrations that:
1. Look like they came from a **beautiful, professionally published children's Bible storybook**
2. Depict African peoples with **warmth, dignity, and cultural specificity**
3. Feel like they were painted by the **same artist** (consistent style across all 23 images)
4. Are **geographically and historically grounded** (correct landscapes, architecture, clothing)
5. Tell the story **visually** even without reading the captions
6. Make the parent think "this is the quality I'd expect from a $30 hardcover picture book"
