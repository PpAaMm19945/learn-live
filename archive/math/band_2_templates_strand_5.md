# Band 2 Constraint Templates (Ages 6-9)
## Strand 5: Mathematical Modeling & Systems (Capstone)

*Note: Strand 5 does not have its own independent DAG of capacities. Instead, it consists of capstone tasks that require the integration of at least 3 capacities from at least 2 different strands at the Discern or Own level.*

*The constraint prompt is shown to the parent. The parent can use it to lead the task, capture it for Async AI, or invoke Live AI.*

---

### Modeling Exemplar 1: The School Garden
**Activation Prerequisites Required:**
1. Strand 1: D4 (Multiplication as Equal Groups) - Execute level
2. Strand 3: G2f (Area — Conceptual) - Encounter level
3. Strand 3: G2c (Standard Measurement) - Execute level

#### Structure
| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Task type** | Structured modeling (Translation to math) |
| **Parent Prompt** | "Say: 'We are designing a tiny garden box. It must be exactly 3 feet wide and 5 feet long. We want to plant carrots, and each carrot needs 1 square foot of space. How many carrots can we plant? Draw a map of the garden on grid paper and write down the math equation that proves your map is right.'" |
| **Success** | Child successfully translates the word problem into a 3x5 grid drawing (Area model), counts or multiplies to find 15 square feet, and writes the corresponding equation (3 x 5 = 15). |
| **Modeling Check** | "If we wanted to plant 20 carrots instead, how could we change the shape of the garden?" |

### Modeling Exemplar 2: The Fair Party Share
**Activation Prerequisites Required:**
1. Strand 1: D5 (Division as Equal Partitioning) - Execute level
2. Strand 1: D2 (Addition) - Own level
3. Strand 4: P2a (Data Collection) - Execute level

#### Structure
| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Task type** | Data-driven resource allocation |
| **Parent Prompt** | "Say: 'We are having a party. Here are 3 bags of candy: one has 12 pieces, one has 8, and one has 10. There are 5 children coming. First, find out exactly how much candy we have total. Then, tell me if we can share it completely fairly without breaking any pieces. Show your work.'" |
| **Success** | Child adds the quantities (12+8+10 = 30), then divides by 5. Predicts 6 pieces per child. |
| **Modeling Check** | "What if another child knocks on the door and joins the party? How do we fix the math?" |

### Modeling Exemplar 3: Designing the Perfect Block City
**Activation Prerequisites Required:**
1. Strand 3: G2b (3D Shape Recognition) - Execute level
2. Strand 2: B5 (Growing Patterns) - Execute level
3. Strand 1: D9 (Estimation) - Encounter level

#### Structure
| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Task type** | Physical construction with constraint |
| **Materials** | Assorted building blocks |
| **Parent Prompt** | "Say: 'Build a tower pattern. The 1st tower must be exactly 1 cube high. The 2nd tower must be 3 cubes high. The 3rd tower must be 5 cubes high. Before you build the 10th tower, estimate: will you have enough cubes if we only have 20 cubes left in the box? Prove it by figuring out the pattern rule.'" |
| **Success** | Child identifies the growing pattern (+2 odds: 1, 3, 5, 7, 9, 11...). Calculates that the 10th tower would be 19 cubes high, so yes, they have enough. |
| **Modeling Check** | "How did you know it would be 19 without building all 10 towers?" |

### Modeling Exemplar 4: The Misleading Poster
**Activation Prerequisites Required:**
1. Strand 4: P2b (Bar Graphs) - Discern level
2. Strand 4: P2c (Asking Good Questions) - Execute level
3. Strand 1: D6 (Number Line) - Execute level

#### Structure
| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Task type** | Critical analysis of generated models |
| **Parent Prompt** | "Show a bar graph that claims 'Kids love spinach!' but the graph only asked 3 kids, and the Y-axis starts at 2 instead of 0. Ask: 'This poster is trying to prove that spinach is the best food. If you were a scientist checking their work, what two questions would you ask about how they made this graph to prove they are cheating?'" |
| **Success** | Child points out the small sample size ("Who did you ask?") and the broken axis/scale ("Why doesn't the line start at zero?"). |
| **Modeling Check** | "Draw what the graph *should* look like if they wanted to be honest." |
